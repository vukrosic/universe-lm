# 186 — Within-Block V-Carry (Per-Head Learnable Recurrence on V) (taste)

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: accept

- **Mechanism is sharp and identity-clean.** Per-head scalar `α_h = tanh(α_raw_h)` with `α_raw_h = 0` init ⇒ `α_h = 0` exactly ⇒ `V_h_t = V_h_t` exactly ⇒ attention product unchanged ⇒ softmax unchanged ⇒ forward and backward graphs byte-identical to baseline. The tanh bound (`|α_h| < 1` strictly) keeps the geometric-sum `1 + α + α² + …` finite for any reachable α, killing the explosion concern. +48 params total (H=4, L=12) — ~+0.005% of 0.94M, basically free. Step-0 byte-identity claim is the load-bearing requirement; the plan names the test (`max_abs_diff(trt_step0_logits, ctrl_step0_logits) == 0.0`) which is the right self-check.

- **Crisp, falsifiable bet, named against a known WIN.** "154's WIN established the K/V rebase axis at 0.94M, and 186 is the *learned dynamic* version of V rebase — if the V-axis reorientation is the binding part of 154's WIN, the optimizer should find a *better* V rebase by learning a per-head time-dependent carry." This is a one-sentence directional prediction with a clear null interpretation: a null at 0.94M tells us the static rebase (154) is the binding part, not the dynamic integration. Win/null both log-worthy. Passes the vibe bar cleanly.

- **Leverage is real, not a rounding-error lever.** 186 changes the attention algebra: once `α_h` drifts off zero, `softmax(QK^T) V` becomes a structurally different operator (V is no longer independent per time step). It's not a tweak of 154 — the math diverges the moment any α is nonzero. Closest analog (154) gave `Δ=−3.48` at 0.94M; 186's lever is even richer (per-head, time-dependent, learnable) so the prior on positive Δ is non-trivial.

- **Information value is high and unique in the queue.** The 021/154 V-side cluster has established that the *V-axis* binds at 0.94M (Q-side 164 nulled at 3.6× plan bar — "V is special"). Within-block V-side is the orthogonal placement (vs 021's cross-block V-residual), and per-head learned α is the orthogonal parameterization (vs 168's fixed `α=0.999` cross-block AV carry, which nulled). A clean null for 186 closes the within-block V-carry axis *and* corroborates the 168 null (recurrence on V-axis doesn't bind at 0.94M regardless of placement). A win unlocks a per-head time-recurrent V lever for Phase-2 ≥135M where the per-head gradient signal is larger and the optimizer can find a richer mix of temporal ranges.

- **Distinct from every closed/active V-axis lever, on the right axes.** 154 = static rebase, both K and V. 168 = cross-block AV carry, fixed α. 164 = cross-block Q carry, fixed α. 021 = cross-block V residual, identity init. 163 = post-attn depthwise conv on V (different family — token-mixing prior, not temporal recurrence). 186 = within-block V carry, per-head learned α, tanh-bounded. All four axes of variation (placement, tensor, parameterization, init) differ from the closed list — the dedup passes the spirit test, not just the literal-keyword test.

- **Niche fit is clean.** Mechanism (not HP) ✓. Identity-zero-init-able ✓. tiny1m3m-runnable ✓. Param budget trivial ✓. `transfer-risk: med` is the right tag — the within-block V-carry form is novel at ≥100M (no published paper tests exactly this), but the underlying recurrence-on-V is well-validated by linear attention at 1B+ and by 021's cross-block V carry at 0.94M. Mechanistic argument (V-axis reorientation is the binding part of 154's WIN) is sound; the per-head axis is a free upgrade on top of a closed lever. The miner's `## Scale evidence` section is present and the `transfer-risk: med` tag is correctly placed.

- **Portfolio check passes.** Active queue (184, 185, 187) is mixed — 184 is logit-scale, 185 is static K-rotation, 187 is lm-head-bias. None of them touch the temporal-V axis. The recent reject cluster (178-180) is per-head multiplicative gates on attention shape, not temporal carry on V. 186 is mechanically orthogonal — different tensor (V), different axis (time), different parameterization (per-head learned α_h). Not crowding the family.

- **The 168 null is a real concern but the lever is structurally different.** Both 168 and 186 are "recurrence on a V-side tensor." But: (a) 168 is cross-block, 186 is within-block (different placement — 168 needs 12 sequential blocks to develop signal, 186 develops within one block); (b) 168 has fixed `α=0.999`, 186 has per-head learned α (different parameterization — the optimizer can choose a useful α, or learn that the useful α is ~0 and the lever is a no-op); (c) 168 carries *AV* (the post-W_O output), 186 carries *V* (pre-softmax, pre-W_O). The mechanism is "recurrence on a V-side tensor" at the highest level, but the lever forms are different enough that a 168 null is not a 186 null. The miner's NULL case explicitly leans on this distinction.

- **DRIFT risk is real but bounded and detectable.** `α_h → 0.99` with `T=2048` gives a 100-step effective window, which may dilute per-step V signal. But (a) the tanh bound prevents `|α| ≥ 1` strictly — no explosion; (b) the implementer should log per-head `|α_h|` at end of training so a DRIFT is interpretable (which heads drifted, by how much); (c) the bar is honest: `Δ > +0.01` is DRIFT, not a failure of the protocol. Tanh's saturation near `±1` provides a soft regularization that keeps α from running away.

- **Caveats the definition gate must weigh.** (a) **Sequential scan is the implementation trap.** A Python for-loop over T=2048 is too slow (~2k sequential ops per head per block per step). The miner correctly flags the `cumsum`-style parallelization: `cumV_t = Σ_{s≤t} α^{t-s} · V_s` is a single associative scan / O(T) parallel op (well-trodden in linear-attention kernels, e.g. fla-org). The implementer must use a vectorized scan — not a Python loop. (b) **Step-0 byte-identity must be verified at all 12 blocks**, not just block 0, because the gate is allocated per block and any parameter-typo (e.g. wrong init for one block) shows up as a step-0 drift. (c) **Log per-head `|α_h|` at end of training** as the primary interpretability signal — without it, a null is uninterpretable (did the optimizer not move α? did it move α to 0? did it diverge per-head?). (d) **Padding / causal boundary**: at `t=0`, the carry has no `V_{t-1}` — the plan correctly sets `V_new[:, :, 0, :] = V[:, :, 0, :]` (zero carry at the left edge), no special token needed. Verify this doesn't break step-0 byte-identity. (e) **Per-head α_h readout is the secondary signal.** Primary metric is still val loss; per-head α_h at end-of-training is the *interpretability* signal that turns a clean null into a mechanistic close.

- **Verdict: accept.** Sharp bet, clean step-0 baseline, structurally distinct from the closed V-axis cluster, information-dense either way, and the per-head learned α is a real upgrade over both 154's static rebase and 168's fixed-α carry. The 168 null is a reason to set the prior on null a bit higher than usual, not a reason to skip the test. Slot earned. Round reset to 1 for the definition loop.
