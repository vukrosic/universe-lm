## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: revise

The lever targets the right axis (low-rank W_O, from-scratch) but the queue already owns this axis, the design is structurally weaker than the existing owner, and the niche-fit math doesn't favor running a duplicate. Three findings, the first one fatal to shipping this as-is.

- **CRITICAL — duplicate of 207-wo-lowrank-bottleneck (needs-plan, approved r1 2026-06-15).** 207 is *the same axis*: a learned low-rank correction on W_O. 199 replaces W_O with a hard low-rank factorization (`A·B`); 207 adds a learnable rank-r residual correction (`W_O_eff = W_O + α·A·B`, α init 0). Both start at full-rank W_O at step 0 (199 via SVD init; 207 via α≈0). Both expose the optimizer to a rank-constrained path on W_O. The review log on 207 explicitly approved the *rank* axis on W_O as "genuinely new" against 171/160/142/197/199-spectral/203. Adding 199 to the queue *is* queue-occupancy on an axis the gate just accepted for someone else. The 5th-optimizer-momentum rule applies: a duplicate of a recently-accepted lever is `revise` even if individually fine.

- **199's "hard-constraint" framing is structurally weaker than 207's "soft-constraint" residual.** Three reasons, in order of weight:
   1. **Bit-identity is engineering-trickier and not actually necessary for the test.** 199's SVD-init route requires decomposing a "ghost" full-rank W_O, splitting Σ between A and B, and surviving the fact that the optimizer *destroys* the factorization (A and B move independently → rank grows beyond r) — so the "low-rank" property is not preserved past step 0 in any meaningful sense, only at step 0. 207's α=0 init is byte-identical for free, with no decomposition plumbing, and the rank-r residual is a clean knob the optimizer either grows or keeps silent.
   2. **The hard-constraint confound.** 199 with r=16 saves 2048 params (−0.22% of 0.94M). Any result is confounded: a "win" could be the rank constraint, the param reduction, or the *combination*; a "null" could be the rank is fine but −0.22% param cut hurt. 207 holds the param count constant (+2.6%, easily inside the noise band) and isolates the *rank hypothesis* from the *param hypothesis*. 207 is the cleaner A/B.
   3. **The 199-SVD-init "byte-identical" framing is misleading.** SVD init makes step-0 forward identical to baseline, but the SVD itself is over a *random* W_O at init (since W_O is fresh), and the *next* training step is the first time the random draws of A, B diverge from the standard W_O. The lever is "different random init that happens to match W_O at step 0" — which is byte-identity at step 0 only by a frozen-W_O-during-construction trick. The same 207 lever achieves step-0 identity with a single scalar α at zero. The "rank axis" is the only thing that matters; both levers probe it; 207 probes it more cleanly.

- **The "capacity-reduction" angle doesn't rescue 199 from the queue-crowding rule.** The bet argues 117/118/146/156 closed because they *added* capacity, and 199 *reduces* it. That's a fair mechanism argument, but it does not differentiate 199 from 207 (which also tests a rank axis on W_O, also reduces *effective* capacity, also forces a structured attention output). The 117/118/146/156 closed-out family is *FFN-shape* capacity injection (MoE, sparse FFN, parallel experts) — that's a different sub-block, and the negative evidence applies to "MoE-style gating at 0.94M," not to "W_O rank at 0.94M." 207 already captured the only direct test of "W_O rank at 0.94M" the queue will run.

- **Niche-fit otherwise OK in principle.** Mechanism (yes — rank-r factorization is a structural lever, not HP). Identity/zero-init-able (yes — both SVD init and Kaiming init are zero-init-able at the parameter level; step-0 byte-identity is achievable). tiny1m3m runnable. Transfer-risk: med is fair (LoRA at 7B-65B, intrinsic-dim literature). The lever clears the niche check, but the niche check does not save it from being a duplicate of an accepted in-flight idea.

- **Crisp bet is missing the strongest prior.** The "Why it's worth a slot" paragraph cites 117/118/146/156 and the LoRA paper, but does not engage with the active queue — specifically 207 (approved 2026-06-15, status `needs-plan`, definition gate cleared), 194-r2 (in repitch to W_V, 2026-06-15, complementary axis to 207), 197-tied-wo-across-blocks (in-queue W_O sharing axis), or 199-spectral-attn-output (in-queue W_O Lipschitz axis). Building a W_O low-rank pitch on 2026-06-15 without citing 207's approval 30 minutes earlier is a stale-queue read. A sharp bet must engage the *current* queue, not just the closed list.

- **What the reviser must do to make this sharp.** Three viable directions; the reviser picks one:
   1. **Pivot to W_Q or W_K** (the two remaining d_model × d_model attention sub-blocks not yet owned by anyone in the active queue). 194-r2 took W_V; 207 took W_O (soft-residual); 199-spectral took W_O (Lipschitz); 197 took W_O (sharing). W_Q and W_K are *open*. The 016-qk_norm WIN shows pre-softmax QK magnitude binds, but 162 (Q-only norm, null) and 165 (K-only norm, null) show single-side norm doesn't fire — single-side *rank* on W_Q or W_K is a different axis (intrinsic rank, not pre-softmax magnitude) and the only one not yet tested at 0.94M.
   2. **Pivot to the residual-stream output (post-MLP)** — same lever shape, different sub-block. Less crowded, but the residual stream post-MLP is rarely a single d_model × d_model projection (the design would need a different shape than `A·B`); lower priority than (1).
   3. **Commit to the param-reduction story as a *complementary* test to 207** (hard constraint vs soft residual, isolates "low-rank W_O for memory savings" from "rank hypothesis binding"). This is the weakest of the three because the marginal info value is small (207 already tests the rank axis, param-saving is a side effect not a primary lever), and the run would need to be defended on the *param* axis, not the *quality* axis. Pass-bar would need to be tightened (must show `Δval ≤ −0.01` AND `−0.22%` params, not just the val-loss bar). Likely not worth a slot.

- **Verdict: revise** — the lever is on a real axis (W_O low-rank) but the axis is owned by 207 (definition-gate approved 2026-06-15, status `needs-plan`); 199's hard-constraint design is structurally weaker than 207's soft-residual on the same axis; the bet doesn't engage 207/194-r2/197/199-spectral. With one of the three pivots above (W_Q, W_K, or a *complementary* param-isolation framing), the lever could re-pitch as a sharp differentiator; without, the next round is a `reject`.

Routing: `needs-repitch` for the miner to pick a W_Q/W_K pivot or commit to a complementary param-isolation framing that engages 207 directly.
