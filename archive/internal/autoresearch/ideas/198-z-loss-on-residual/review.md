## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: approve

- **Source is real and the residual-side form is a defensible composition of a well-validated primitive.** PaLM logit z-loss (Chowdhery et al. 2022, arXiv:2204.02311) is a citable, scale-validated regularizer on a magnitude-related quantity — the lever's *family* is real. The "Stability of Large Language Models (various 2024 papers)" citation is vague (not a single arXiv id), but the repitch is honest about it: residual-side z-loss `log(1+||r||²)` is explicitly framed as a "compositional" variant that has not been directly tested at scale, and the mechanism is well-motivated as the *complementary* axis to logit z-loss. Gemma 2's attention-logit softcap (Team et al. 2024) is the closest real-world analogue on the *stability* axis. Source realism: acceptable; not a fabricated citation, the gap is the standard "natural variant" framing.
- **Mechanism is a mechanism, not an HP lever.** Adding `z_coef · log(1+||r||²).mean()` to the loss is a *loss-side* regularizer — it changes the gradient w.r.t. every parameter in the backbone, not just an LR/schedule/init-constant. Step-0 byte-identity holds: residual `O(√L)` at step 0 gives `z_coef · log(1+O(L)) · 1` contribution, which for `z_coef=1e-4`, `L=12` is `O(1e-3) ≪ ce_loss ≈ 9.0`. 0 new params, 1 forward hook, 1 config flag, 1 loss term. The implementation pattern is already established in the repo by 167-logit-zloss (`getattr(config, "use_z_loss", False)` / `getattr(config, "z_loss_lambda", 0.0)`), so the reviser can follow the 167 path and just swap the L2 penalty computation. <200 LoC: trivially yes (~5-10 LoC in the forward + 1 config block + 1 trainer.py branch).
- **Tiny1m3m only.** The plan, the falsification signature, the gradient-magnitude numbers (`d_model=64`, `√d_model=8`, `n_layers=12`), and the residual-norm trace all reference tiny1m3m (0.94M · 3M tok, seed 42). No `screen20m`, no ladder, no multi-tier. Scope-clean.
- **Not already closed — the residual-side axis is a distinct lever from the closed logit-side and other stability levers.** 167-logit-zloss (null Δ=-0.0018) is the *direct* sibling but targets `log(1+max_logit²)`, mediated by the LM head and attenuated by `1/√d_model` in the gradient path. 198 targets `log(1+||r||²)`, a *direct* gradient on the residual stream with no LM-head attenuation. 110/122/124/134 (optimizer-side, tier-mismatch), 111-drop-path (stochastic-depth regularizer, not magnitude), 017-sub-ln-sandwich / 130-rezero / 142-layerscale (per-block learned depth-conditional scalars, not loss-side), 196-block-residual-ema (taste-rejected, EMA-mixing axis with detach+learnable gate, not a static L2 penalty) — none of these are the same axis. The repitch correctly identifies 167 as the *closest* empirical prior and explicitly argues why residual-side may bind where logit-side nulled (the residual's magnitude is *not* init-bounded the way the LM head's output is). The differentiation from active siblings 195 (mid-attn RMSNorm, attention-side) and 197 (init-time α-scale, init-time) is cleanly partitioned: 195=placement/post-product QK, 197=init-time residual scale, 198=training-time loss penalty. Three orthogonal sub-levers on the broader magnitude-stability axis.
- **Falsifiable pass/fail bar is the strongest part of the repitch — and the reviser must preserve it verbatim into the plan.** The pre-registered `||r||_L2` trace at steps `{0, 100, 500, 1000, 2000}` with three regimes (binding ≥40, well-conditioned ≤24, indeterminate 24-40) is exactly what the taste r1 review asked for. A WIN requires `mean(||r||_L2) ≥ 5·√d_model = 40` at step 2k *and* `trt_val ≤ ctrl_val − 0.005`; a NULL with `||r||_L2 ≤ 24` is the *symmetric axis closure* to 167 (informative); a NULL with `||r||_L2 ≥ 40` is a *steered NULL* and would warrant a follow-up at `z_coef=1e-3`. The 167-comparison prediction (residual L2 gradient `∂penalty/∂||r|| = 2·||r||/(1+||r||²) ≈ 0.05` at `||r||=40`, vs 167's gradient attenuated by `1/√d_model=1/8` through the LM head) is a concrete, falsifiable, mechanistic argument — not a vibe. The cache reference (champion val ≈ 6.24, cache baseline 6.40) and the two-ctrl rule are correctly applied.
- **Transfer-risk: low is correct and justified.** §Scale evidence cites PaLM at 8B-540B for the logit-side form and Gemma 2 at 2B-27B for the stability axis. The mechanism is a generic L2 penalty on the residual stream — not scale-bound, not tied to tiny-vocab or 3M-token regime. At 12L, residual growth is moderate; at 24L+ in the 135M recipe, the lever's leverage grows because the residual stream's natural magnitude is larger, so the penalty term becomes more binding. This is the right directionality for "low" risk: a small lever at this tier with *growing* leverage at scale.
- **One non-gating observation for the reviser, to encode in the plan's instrumentation, not in the mechanism.** The repitch computes `res_norm = final_residual.norm(dim=-1)` which is the per-token L2 norm over the `d_model=64` axis. ✓ This is the right shape (per-token, not per-batch). Mean over `(B, T)` is the right reduction. The trainer should log `res_norm.detach().mean().item()` and `res_norm.detach().max().item()` per step so the trace committed in the falsification signature is *actually observable in the run logs*, not retroactively inferred from a checkpoint. (Same pattern as 167's `z_loss_val` logging at `trainer.py:1706` — the reviser should follow that.)
