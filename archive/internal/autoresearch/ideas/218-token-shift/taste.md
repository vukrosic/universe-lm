# Taste log — 218 token-shift

## r1 — 2026-06-16 — verdict: accept
- **Axis-completion bet on a known-suggestive family.** 143-shortconv (pre-attn depthwise k=3 causal Conv1d on V, *borderline null* — beats 4/4 same-day ctrls by Δ 0.05–0.12 but the two-ctrl bracket gap of 0.07 trips the formal WIN rule) and 163-v-mix-conv (post-attn depthwise k=3 on V, *clean null*) are the two closed positions. 218 places the *same mechanism* on Q and K — a third, **mathematically non-equivalent** tensor position: V mixes the already-weighted attention output (so the conv is mixing post-softmax weighted V), whereas Q and K feed the softmax directly, so a small local mix is a *soft locality prior on the attention pattern itself*. This is the more interesting of the three positions because 143's borderline result suggests the family has signal but the right axis is unknown. A WIN would localize the gain to Q/K pre-softmax; a NULL would close the third axis at 0.94M and let us move on. Both outcomes are informative.
- **Sharp sentence.** "We expect Δval ≈ −0.01 to −0.03 because pre-smoothing Q and K with a 3-token neighborhood gives a cheap soft-locality prior to the global attention softmax, while the global attention softmax at 0.94M / T=2048 is sharp enough that a small prior can re-weight the distribution without breaking the global pass." The miner also names the null hypothesis cleanly: "global attention pass already absorbs local structure at 0.94M" — same null hypothesis 143 and 163 were tested against, but on a different tensor where it might fail differently.
- **Niche fit is clean.** Mechanism (not HP), zero-init-able (bit-identical step 0), tiny1m3m-able, +72 scalars total (+0.008% of 0.94M, well under the 1% "free" envelope), 24 convs across 12 layers (2 per block × 12). The conv weights are 1-D kernels — route them to AdamW mirroring 191-token-attn-gain's per-tensor pattern.
- **Transfer risk `low` is justified.** Hyena (Poli et al. 2023, arXiv:2302.10866, 1B–7B), MEGABYTE (Yu et al. 2023, arXiv:2305.07185, 250M–1.5B local-conv patch encoder), StripedHyena (DeepMind 2023, 7B) all validate local conv on Q/K/V at >100M source scale. The mechanism is a single linear op on a single tensor — scale-agnostic by construction. Distinct from the full Hyena *replacement* (which is tier-mismatched) — 218 is the *residual additive* form (gate=0 / zero init ⇒ starts as baseline) that is the same flavor StripedHyena uses.
- **Distinct from prior closed levers, and the prior LEAK.** 143 (pre-attn, V), 157 (post-FFN-activation, depthwise), 163 (post-attn, V), 180-qk-logit-conv (on the *scores*, LEAK — broken causal mask). 218 is on Q and K *vectors* pre-softmax with a properly causal left-pad of length 2, NOT on the scores. 180's LEAK was causal-mask on the attention scores; 218's mask is on the Conv1d input where `manual left-padding of length 2` for a kernel of 3 is a well-defined pattern. The definition gate must verify the step-0 byte-identity claim, but the lever is a different operation on different tensors.
- **Caveats for the definition gate (not blocking).**
  - **Causal padding must be done by manual left-pad of length k−1=2, not by `padding=k//2` in nn.Conv1d.** Symmetric padding would let the conv see the future token at the boundary and produce a non-bit-identical step-0. The miner already specifies manual left-pad; pin it in `plan.md`.
  - **Conv must be constructed as a raw `nn.Parameter(zeros(d_model, 1, 3))` (or equivalent explicit-init path) — NOT `nn.Conv1d(…)` followed by `.data.zero_()`.** Constructing `nn.Conv1d` consumes RNG via `kaiming_uniform_`, shifting the RNG state for every subsequent block's `q_proj`/`k_proj` random init and breaking the step-0 byte-identity claim across blocks 2..12. Same fix the 157/163 plans enforce. Pin the fp32 max-abs-diff < 1e-6 self-check across all 12 blocks at the no-flag path vs the flag-on path *with* the explicit-init construction.
  - **Q and K each get a separate conv (24 total at 12L × 2).** Do NOT share weights across Q and K — they sit on different gradients and tying them would create a phantom cross-coupling the optimizer would have to undo. Mirrors the closed `191-token-attn-gain` structure (per-tensor 1-D kernel on the head optimizer).
  - **Route the conv weights to AdamW (or whichever optimizer handles 1-D per-channel params in this codebase).** The head optimizer pattern from 191 is the reference. Tiny param count (72 scalars) means this is a routing concern, not a memory concern.
  - **No interaction with the active champion stack** (175-alibi + 154-rebased + 016-qk_norm + 021-value-residual): the 218 lever is pre-softmax Q/K mix, and the existing levers sit on K-side normalization (016), K/V rebiasing (154), attention output (021), and ALiBi slopes (175) — none touch Q or K with a learned local mix before the dot product. Verify the plan cites the exact apply site in `models/layers.py` (`MultiHeadAttention.forward`, after `Q = self.q_proj(x)` and `K = self.k_proj(x)`, before the head reshape).
  - **Plan bar**: Δ ≤ −0.01 = WIN, |Δ| < 0.01 = NULL band, Δ > +0.01 = DRIFT. Two-ctrl bracket at tiny1m3m; |Δ| inside ±0.04 noise band of the baseline cache (6.4394±0.04) is the actual gate.
- **Accept and route to definition gate.** `round` resets to 1 for the definition loop.
