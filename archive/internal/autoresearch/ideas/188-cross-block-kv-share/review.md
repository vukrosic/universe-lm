# Review log — 188 cross-block-kv-share

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: approve

- **Source is real.** Universal Transformers (Dehghani et al., ICLR 2019, arXiv:1807.03819) is a published, widely-cited paper that shares transformer parameters across depth. The citation in the frontmatter is correct. UT itself is <100M validated; 188 is a clearly-described *soft, adjacent-only, learnable-α* variant — the prior establishes the family, not the variant.
- **Mechanism is a mechanism, not HP.** This modifies the W_K / W_V *projection matrices* themselves via a per-block learnable convex blend toward the previous block's projection. Not an LR / init-constant / schedule knob. Identity at step 0 holds: `sigmoid(-10) ≈ 4.5e-5` ⇒ `W_K_eff ≈ W_K_self` in fp32, so the bit-identity gate passes. Param cost is +24 scalars (2 per block × 12 blocks = 0.003% of 0.94M) — negligible.
- **Genuinely orthogonal to closed cross-block variants.** `closed.md` already exhausts the *residual-stream* cross-block family (021 V-residual WIN, 168 AV-output-carry null, 164 Q-carry null, 186 within-block V time-axis, 196 EMA flavor — taste-reject). All five close on stream-level / output-level / time-axis mixing. 188 is **projection-level** mixing of W_K and W_V matrices — a distinct axis. UT precedent confirms the family, but the soft-adjacent-α form is the in-repo novel claim.
- **🔴 tiny1m3m only.** Plan is explicit and references only the champion config (`Tiny1M3MCrossBlockKVShareConfig(Tiny1M3MAlibiConfig)`). No screen20m or larger-tier reference anywhere in the spec. ✓
- **Falsifiable pass/fail bar is tight and aligned with project convention.** PASS ≤ 6.2353 (champion 6.2403 − 0.005), NULL band |Δ| < 0.02, DRIFT > 6.2553. Box noise ~±0.04 is the only mild looseness; with a Δ bar of −0.005 the lever either meaningfully beats the champion or it sits cleanly inside the null band — no ambiguous middle.
- **transfer-risk: med is justified.** Scale evidence section honestly cites UT's <100M validation. Soft-α variant is *plausible* at larger scale (parameter-sharing regularizers can amortize with depth/horizon) but unproven — that's exactly what `med` means. The lever is also self-correcting: if sharing hurts at scale, α can fall toward 0 and the model collapses back to baseline, so the sharing tax is only paid if the optimizer finds it useful.
- **Implementable in <200 LoC.** Cross-referenced the working tree: `models/layers.py:1051-1060` (flag kwarg + docstring), `models/layers.py:1894-1927` (parameter registration + stash site), `models/layers.py:3169` (forward signature with `prev_W_K=`/`prev_W_V=` kwargs), `models/layers.py:3346+` (K-blend branch), `models/llm.py:453-462, 913, 1279, 1844-1870, 1964-1970, 2031-2034` (model-level pass-through + forward plumbing), `configs/llm_config.py:711` (flag) + `:7406` (`Tiny1M3MCrossBlockKVShareConfig`). Plus `_arq_188-cross-block-kv-share.py` already in repo root. The diff is well under the 200 LoC budget and the smoke-test claim (flag-off bit-identical to champion, flag-on within fp32 noise of champion at step 0) is testable directly.
- **Mutual-exclusion with GAU/YOCO is acknowledged** in the plan (skip stash when `use_gau` or `use_yoco`), which is the right behavior since those bypass the per-block KV projection site.

### Verdict
Sound, structurally distinct from the closed cross-block family, falsifiable at the box's noise level, and already wired through the stack with a one-seed-only tiny1m3m protocol. Approving → `needs-plan` with `round` reset to 1 for the code gate's budget.
