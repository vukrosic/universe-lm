## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: accept

- **Crisp bet present.** "At 0.94M with tied embeddings, the per-token output logit is fully coupled to the per-token input embedding (a 64-dim vector shared between input and output); a separate per-vocab additive bias gives the optimizer a direct scalar output knob that doesn't disturb the input." This is a falsifiable, scale-relevant mechanism — not a vibe.
- **Leverage is real at this tier.** With `Embedding.T @ final_residual`, the per-token output logit IS `Embedding[v] · final_residual`. Updating `Embedding[v]` along `final_residual`'s direction is a 64-component gradient move to achieve what an additive bias does in 1 component. A per-vocab bias is a strictly more parameter-efficient axis at 0.94M. With 92 update steps × ~3M tokens of gradient signal, the bias has enough data to shift the most common tokens meaningfully; rare tokens stay pinned near 0 (a built-in sparsity-of-effect — the lever self-limits to where the data lives).
- **Fresh axis — no prior lever tests per-vocab LM-head bias.** Defensive comparison against the closed axes holds: not 159 (input-side LN, DRIFT), not 167 (logit regularizer, null), not 166/152 (attention-side logit bias, null), not the closed logit-softcap axis. Distinct architectural location.
- **Transfer-risk: low is correct.** T5 220M-11B validates the lever form at ≥100M. The 2023+ frontier-decoder abandonment of the bias is *not* a transfer-failure argument — it's an argument that the lever has small marginal value when the model has enough capacity to absorb the per-token shift directly into the embedding. At 0.94M that capacity is the binding constraint, so the lever is *most* useful here without being only-useful-here. Reasonable to carry toward 135M.
- **Portfolio fit acceptable.** Two output-head levers in flight (183 pre-LM-head RMSNorm, 184 learned logit scale), but 187 is mechanistically orthogonal — a per-vocab 1-D parameter rather than a shared scalar or normalization. Not crowding; cluster of *distinct* output-head probes is fine.
- **Both outcomes informative.** Win → unlocks a per-vocab bias lever for the 135M recipe. Null → closes the LM-head-bias axis at 0.94M, validates the tied-no-bias frontier convention at our tier, frees budget.
