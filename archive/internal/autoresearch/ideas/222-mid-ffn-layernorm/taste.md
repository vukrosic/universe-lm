## r1 — 2026-06-16 — verdict: accept
- **Novel placement, not a re-skin.** Closed axes the spirit of this could match are 017-sub-ln-sandwich (extra norm *around* block, 6L, null) and 142-layerscale (per-channel gain *on residual*, 12L, null) — both norm-related, both null at our tier. But both are block-level; 222 is FFN-*internal* between up-proj and down-proj. The 153-relu2-ffn and 170-swiglu-ffn nulls closed the FFN-activation and FFN-gating axes at 0.94M, but neither is a *normalization* lever — so 222 is the third FFN-internal axis, not a duplicate of either.
- **Per-block, not depth-compounding.** 017/142 fail at 6L/12L partly because the depth axis doesn't accumulate; 222 is purely per-block (12 independent FFN-internal LNs at d_ff=256, +6,144 params / +0.65%), so the depth-conditional null pattern from the DeepNet/Sub-LN family does not apply. Bit-identical at step 0 (gain=1, bias=0) — clean A/B, zero step-0 risk.
- **Sharp bet, both directions informative.** Win ⇒ "FFN mid-state distribution is a binding constraint at 0.94M, learnable internal normalizer helps" (an axis no prior at our tier has tested). Null ⇒ "FFN mid-state is well-behaved as-is, internal normalizers are redundant with the pre-FFN RMSNorm" — closes the FFN-internal-normalization axis at our tier alongside the 153/170 closes on activation/gating.
- **Niche fit: clean.** Mechanism (gain/bias on a learned affine inside FFN, not a HP). Identity-init-able (gain=1, bias=0). Cheap (1 RMSNorm per FFN × 12 blocks, ~15 LoC). transfer-risk=med is the right call — source papers test at 100M+, so a 0.94M null is the expected default, but a 0.94M win would be a real signal the FFN mid-state dominates at this tier.
