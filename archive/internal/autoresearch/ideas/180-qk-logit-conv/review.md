# Review log — 180 qk-logit-conv

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: approve
- **Source is real and honestly bounded.** Conformer (Gulati 2020, arXiv:2005.08100) and ConvBERT (Jiang 2020, arXiv:2008.02450) are real; both verified. The miner correctly flags that *neither* validates the exact QK^T placement — Conformer is conv-on-x and ConvBERT is conv-on-Q/K. There is no published paper at this exact form, and the author says so plainly. med transfer-risk is the honest tag.
- **Mechanism is structural, not HP.** 1-D causal depthwise conv on `QK^T / √d_k` along the key axis is a *function-class* lever (the kernel can express smoothing, sharpening, asymmetric shift, or anything in between). None of the closed per-head-attention-shape levers (152 logit-bias, 155 per-head-temp, 160 post-AV gain, 162 Q-only-norm, 165 K-only-norm, 166 T5-RPE) are convolutions — they are scalars, biases, or RMSNorms. 143-shortconv is conv on the residual stream `x` *before* QKV projection (different site, different gradient path). 154-rebased-attn is the closest cousin (per-head K/V rebase, pre-softmax, recently-WIN Δ≈-3.48) but it modifies K and V rather than the dot-product output. The QK^T placement is not a duplicate.
- **Step-0 byte-identical claim is verifiable.** `w_h = [0, 0, 1]` delta init ⇒ `scores_conv = scores` ⇒ softmax unchanged. With kernel pinned to delta at construction, the lever reproduces baseline fp32-bit-exact at step 0 (max-abs-diff = 0.0). 144 params (H=4 × K=3 × n_layers=12, +0.015% of 0.94M) — trivial.
- **<200 LoC, fits the existing lever pattern.** The implementation slots in cleanly next to the existing score-side levers (152, 155, 166, 169): ~30 LoC in `models/layers.py` for the MHA branch + the grouped conv, ~5 LoC in `configs/llm_config.py` for the flag + tiny1m3m subclass override, ~5 LoC in `models/llm.py` for threading. Well under the budget.
- **Niche fit solid; not crowding the queue.** The miner is right that 180 is the *first* per-head-attention-*kernel* lever in flight (not the 7th per-head-scalar lever) and that 154-rebased-attn's WIN is the freshest signal that locality-prior levers bind at this tier. A null would close the logit-smoothing axis; a win would unlock a fresh logit-space placement for Phase-2.
- **Plan must set a numerical pass/fail bar at tiny1m3m.** The idea's "why it's worth a slot" lays out the bet qualitatively but does not pin a number. The cache baseline is 6.4394±0.04 (per the 152/154/155/166 cluster). A reasonable bar: `|Δ| < 0.01` ⇒ NULL (right-sign or wrong-sign inside noise band); `Δ ≤ -0.01` ⇒ WIN. Plan.md should state this and the WIN trigger separately from the bar. Cache-authoritative (same as 154/166/170).
- **Implementation guidance for code gate.** (a) Use `F.conv1d` with **grouped conv** over B*H groups (one kernel per head), kernel size 3, padding=2 with manual left-only padding (causal), or equivalently unfold+pad-0. Avoid a Python `for h in H` loop — it's 4× slower at 12L. (b) Apply the causal mask **after** the conv: `conv → masked_fill(-inf) → softmax`. The kernel still receives gradient on unmasked positions and masked-out positions stay masked. (c) Pin the kernel center to 1.0 at init via a direct `self.logit_conv_w[:, 1] = 1.0` assignment after the zero init, and leave it free to drift. (d) Self-check must verify fp32 max-abs-diff < 1e-6 vs the no-flag baseline across all 12 blocks (not just block 0) before declaring step-0 OK.
- **One note on the parameterization sketch in idea.md:36-43.** The "w_h[0] = -w_pos_raw_h, w_h[1] = 1 + w_diag_raw_h, w_h[2] = w_neg_raw_h" sign-asymmetry hint is a fine parsimonious reparam but is *not* required for the lever to work. A plain `[w_h[0], w_h[1], w_h[2]]` with `w_h[1]` pinned to 1 at init is equivalent and simpler. Code gate should pick the simpler form unless the asymmetry bet is documented in plan.md.
- **What the result actually means.** NULL ⇒ logit-space per-head smoothing doesn't bind at 0.94M/12L/4H (consistent with 152/155/160/162/165/166 all null in the per-head-attention-shape family). WIN ⇒ logit-space is the right axis to scale up for Phase-2 ≥135M. Either way, the bet is crisp and the implementation is cheap.
