# Taste log — 026 FIRE × QK-Norm

## r1 — 2026-06-10 — verdict: accept
- **Leverage**: real. Both 009 FIRE (Δ −0.064/−0.082, val 6.3234) and 016 QK-Norm (Δ −0.0138/−0.0185, val 6.3906) won at tiny1m3m, seed 42. The composition tests a real bet: do two attention-domain levers superadd, just add, or saturate?
- **Mechanism orthogonality — the load-bearing argument**: FIRE is *additive* (per-position bias onto attention logits post-dot-product). QK-Norm is *multiplicative-normalizing* (LayerNorm on Q,K head-dim, bounding Q·K magnitude). They operate at different points in the forward and on different mathematical axes. The 013-cope DRIFT (+0.143 vs FIRE-alone) is the obvious cautionary prior, but 013's failure was specifically *two additive position-bias levers compounding* — QK-Norm does not compound additively with FIRE's bias; it bounds the magnitude of the Q·K product that the bias gets added to. This is the qualitative difference that makes 026 a different bet from 013.
- **Niche fit**: clean. QK-Norm is a mechanism (per-head logit bounding), identity-init-able (LN γ=1, β=0 starts at baseline), and 016 already proved it fires at tiny1m3m. FIRE is already shipped. Two-flag change in `MHA.forward`. No compute cost worth modeling.
- **Information value is high either way**: a WIN superadditive would mean the two attention stabilizers are independent and the recipe stack is *not* saturated in the attention domain — licenses promoting both as defaults in the 135M carry. Additive (~−0.078) means orthogonal — both carry, no double-count. A null or a *regression* would tell us the 013-cope DRIFT generalises — that the attention domain is *saturated* after FIRE and further attention-logit-adjacent levers are wasted/destructive. That null is the most informative possible result for prioritising the 135M recipe carry.
- **Transfer risk**: low. Both source levers are validated at tiny1m3m and at much larger scale (FIRE: NeurIPS 2023 paper; QK-Norm: Qwen3, SmolLM3, 22B ViT). The composition is identity-safe (LN resets each step).
- **Crisp bet** (for the definition gate): "We expect Δ roughly additive (~−0.014 QK-Norm × ~−0.064 FIRE ≈ −0.078 vs *FIRE-alone*, not vs the older ctrl) if the two levers are mathematically orthogonal. A superadditive (−0.09+) would mean the per-head logit bounding makes FIRE's learned position bias more *consistent* across heads. A null or regression would mean 013-cope generalises: attention-domain headroom is exhausted by FIRE at this scale."
- **Definition-gate notes** (carried forward, not blocking):
  - The control must be **FIRE-alone** (current best baseline at val 6.3234), not the original tiny1m3m ctrl. The A/B is "FIRE + QK-Norm vs FIRE-alone" — the additive Δ estimate above is measured against FIRE-alone.
  - Pass bar should be set at the upper end of the hypothesis (≤ −0.01 vs FIRE-alone) so a sub-noise "win" inside the ctrl-to-ctrl gap doesn't get called a win.
  - The 013-cope DRIFT should be cited as the relevant prior in `plan.md`'s risk section, with the explicit mechanism-orthogonality argument for why 026 is a different bet.
- **Portfolio**: 027 (Moonlight × QK-Norm) is a parallel composition test on the *optimizer* axis — different family, no crowding. The active queue's other `needs-taste` ideas (028 deep-thin, 029 V-Norm, 030 U-Net skip) are architecture/config variety, not more compositions. Fine.
- **Verdict**: `accept`. The 013-cope precedent is a warning the definition gate should respect, not a blocker at taste. The mechanism is orthogonally different; the bet is sharp; the information value is high in all three outcome branches.
