- **Source verified.** arXiv:2012.11747 resolves: "RealFormer: Transformer Likes Residual Attention" (He et al., Dec 2020). Mechanism (pre-softmax score residual across adjacent layers, `scores_l += scores_{l-1}`, zero-init cache → step 0 = baseline) matches the paper. Not a fabrication. Authors plausible. 2020 vintage — old but the mechanism is the lever, not the publication recency.
- **Mechanism is real, not a hyperparameter.** Pre-softmax score carry is a structural change in the attention path. Zero-init cache (the spec's pinned init) means step 0 is the plain-softmax baseline, so any Δ at end of training is a learned structural delta, not a wiring artifact. Identity-init property is explicit in idea.md:15. ✅
- **Not a duplicate.** Cross-checked `closed.md` axes (`NoPE, post-norm, layer tying, MHA vs GQA, MLA, Tied QK, …`, `NSA / diff-attn / hybrid heads`, `Multiscale heads / parallel block / attn sink`) — none of them touch the pre-softmax score surface. The 021 / 044 family is the obvious cross-check: **021-value-residual** is a residual on **values** (post-projection, pre-`attn @ V`; closed.md:52, won with caveat), **044-attnres** is on the **residual stream** (depth-axis sum over block updates, currently `repitching` r1) — both are different surfaces. 020-forgetting-attn is post-softmax multiplicative decay, also different. RealFormer is its own lever. ✅
- **tiny1m3m + seed 42 only, no multi-tier.** Idea.md:11 + 19 reference tiny1m3m directly. No `screen20m`, no full-ladder language. ✅

- **Pin numerical pass bar with a number.** Idea says "small but real val-loss drop" — fails the "falsifiable pass/fail bar tied to a real control" check. Use the same shape as 021 (`autoresearch/ideas/021-value-residual/idea.md:38-42`) calibrated to the FIRE-ctrl noise floor (run-to-run ≈ ±0.01, ctrl spread 6.3875–6.4050 ≈ 0.0175 at the 009 family): **WIN** `trt < ctrl − 0.005` (low-to-moderate bar; 6 carryover transitions at L=6 is small but non-zero), **NULL** `|trt − ctrl| < 0.01` (sub-noise; the lever doesn't fire on top of FIRE at this scale), **FAIL** `trt > ctrl + 0.01` (the score carry is hurting, not helping — same failure shape as 021).
- **Pin seed = 42 only.** Add explicit `### Seed` subsection mirroring 021: single fixed seed, no multi-seed sweep, no per-seed mean. A sub-noise delta is inconclusive, not real; never add "run more seeds to confirm" — log null and move on.
- **Pin the score-cache plumbing so the code-implementer doesn't reinvent it.** Spec currently says "cache the previous layer's pre-softmax attention state" + "detached across layers" — that's the *what* but not the *where*. Pin the design: (i) **forward-pass-local** stash on the MHA module (`self._scores_cache = scores.detach()` after the manual-attention path computes the pre-softmax `[B, n_heads, T, T]` score tensor); (ii) the model's `forward()` loop reads `block.attention._scores_cache` after each block call and passes it as a positional kwarg `prev_scores` to the next block's MHA; (iii) layer 0 receives `prev_scores=None` and the spec must say explicitly what to do with `None` (no add — step 0 ≡ baseline, by the zero-init + no-add path together; if the spec wants the belt-and-braces option, layer 0 also initializes its `_scores_cache` to a zero tensor of shape `[B, n_heads, T, T]` and the next layer's add is the only carry, which keeps the shape uniform across layers). Pick (ii)/(None = skip add) for simplicity — fewer LoC, identical math, easier to assert in the test. Mirror the V-residual `_v_residual` plumbing shape (`autoresearch/ideas/021-value-residual/idea.md:24-26`) since the cross-layer carry is the exact same pattern on a different surface.
- **Pin the **detached** property in the Definition, not just Mechanism.** Idea says "cache detached across layers" but doesn't say *where* the detach lives. Spec must say: `self._scores_cache = scores.detach()` so the layer-l add's gradient does not flow back into the layer-(l-1) attention logit computation — each layer's own softmax is trained on its own path; the cross-layer score carry learns only the routing shape. (Same pattern as 021 `.detach()` on the V stash.)
- **Pin the cache shape and site precisely.** The cache is the **pre-softmax, post-mask** attention score tensor, shape `[B, n_heads, T, T]` (where T = `seq_len` of the current forward). Site: **after** the manual-attention path computes `scores = (Q @ K.transpose(-2, -1)) * (1/√d_k)` and **after** the optional `use_cope` / `use_softpick`-style pre-softmax bias add, **before** the softmax (the spec must name the exact line, by analogy with 021 naming `models/layers.py:1380`). At the site the FIRE-equipped baseline already forces the manual-attention path (same as 021), so 071 needs no extra manual-branch forcing — it sits in the same path the FIRE-ctrl already takes. If the spec wants the lever to ALSO fire on a non-FIRE baseline in a future run, name that as a future-work note, not a current-rum bar.
- **LoC budget (≤ 50 LoC, well under 200).** Mirror 021's (a)/(b)/(c)/(d)/(e) decomposition. Estimated breakdown: (a) `use_realformer: bool = False` kwarg on `LLMConfig` + `MultiHeadAttention` + `TransformerBlock` and forward it through the constructor chain (`configs/llm_config.py` + `models/llm.py` + `models/layers.py` — small additions analogous to 021's flags), ~10 LoC; (b) layer-0 MHA stash of `scores.detach()` and outer-loop `prev_scores` plumbing in `models/llm.py:forward()` analogous to 021's `v_residual` plumbing, ~10 LoC; (c) add `if self.use_realformer and prev_scores is not None: scores = scores + prev_scores` in the MHA manual-attention path, right after the `scores = (Q @ K^T) * scale` line and after any optional `use_cope`/`use_softpick` bias add, before the softmax, ~4 LoC; (d) new config class `Tiny1M3MRealFormerOnFireConfig` exported from `configs/__init__.py`, ~5 LoC; (e) step-0 identity test: `use_realformer=False` ≡ baseline at step 0 (already covered by the existing default) AND `use_realformer=True` (with the layer-0 `_scores_cache = scores.detach()` path) ≡ baseline at step 0 within `1e-5` (no carry, no add ⇒ scores are bit-identical), ~10 LoC. Total ≈ 39 LoC, well under 50 and 200.
- **Add an "### Evidence to capture" subsection.** The natural artifact to log: the magnitude of the carryover — i.e. `||scores_{l} - scores_{l-1}||_F` per layer, collected at end of training via the `prev_scores` plumbing (one scalar per block transition, 5 transitions at L=6). A uniform "scores don't change" (carry has near-zero magnitude) is a **stronger null** than "inside variance" — it means the model learned to leave the previous score un-touched. A non-monotonic profile (carry grows with depth, or is non-zero only in middle layers) is itself a finding. Mirror 021's per-block λ readout at `autoresearch/ideas/021-value-residual/idea.md:55-58`.
- **Transfer-risk tag is fine but justify it explicitly.** `transfer-risk: med` is set and the scale-evidence paragraph names BERT/ETC + GLUE/SQuAD/NMT/WikiHop/HotpotQA/NQ/OpenKP — that's the right *evidence* but it doesn't say *what* the published effect size was. Tighten the Scale-evidence paragraph to one sentence on the largest scale (BERT-base, 12L) and one on the smallest scale the paper reports (cite the table — a quick check on the paper's Table 1 numbers would do it; the r2 reviewer will verify). "Published wins are not specifically on a 0.94M causal LM" is correct but is the disclaimer, not the justification — the `med` tag is the *concession*, the lever's deep-vs-shallow story is the *claim*. Re-word to lead with the claim.
- **Not a blocker for the code gate, but worth flagging at plan time.** The 6 carryover transitions at L=6 means the lever's effective "depth" is small — even a successful score carry only modifies 5 transition sites. The expect-Δ band at WIN should reflect that (`-0.005` low, not `-0.02`); the null band `±0.01` is the dominant outcome under the box noise floor. This is already implicit in the pass bar; call it out in the Why paragraph so the runner knows the expected magnitude and doesn't mistake a `-0.003` real-but-tiny win for a null.
