# Taste log — 074 transformer-xl

## r2 — 2026-06-11 — verdict: accept
- **All r1 findings closed.** Segment now defined (chunk=512, 4 sub-segments per 2048-seq forward pass, detached K/V prepended); positional collision resolved by explicit choice (RoPE-extend positions 0..511 cache + 512..1023 current, no Shaw bias, FIRE/RoPE stack untouched); crisp bet stated as Δ -0.01..-0.04 with a mechanism (cold-start patching at sub-segment boundaries); family-distinction from 008/012/004 is real (XL is an *additive* K/V cache on softmax, not a softmax-kernel replacement).
- **Composes with the active stack, not against it.** Sits on top of FIRE (009, Δ -0.064 WIN), V-residual (021, Δ -0.034 WIN w/ caveat), Canon-Conv (023, Δ -0.084 WIN w/ caveat) — all softmax-based wins. The K/V cache extends the sequence the kernel sees; it does not change the kernel. Distinct from the linear-attention replacement family that closed at this tier.
- **In-niche bet at predicted magnitude.** Predicted Δ -0.01..-0.04 lands in the band where 015-Moonlight (-0.0138) and 016-QK-norm (-0.0138) both passed plan bars at tiny1m3m. Zero new learnable params, identity-able at step 0 (first sub-segment of first example has empty cache → bit-exact baseline). Parameter-golf compatible.
- **Informative either way.** A win → opens a memory-recurrence branch (FAM/RMT family) for the 135M recipe. A null → closes "cheap detached segment-memory doesn't fire at 3M tokens / seq_len=2048" and lets the queue drop the entire branch (informs sibling 045-transformerfam's gated-learnable variant of the same question). Not a "meh, inconclusive" failure mode.
- **Portfolio fit OK.** Memory/recurrence family currently has only 074 + 045-transformerfam active; they test the same question (does memory beyond the segment help at this tier?) from complementary angles (param-free detached cache vs gated learnable feedback). Two ideas in this family is not crowded — it's a clean pair.
- **Remaining quibbles are definition-gate work, not taste-gate gaps.** The "effective context 512→1024" framing oversells the gain (SWA(512) still bounds per-query reach — the cache mainly warm-starts the cold positions of sub-segments 2/3/4, not literally 2×-ing context). The "1-in-4 boundaries carry a real cross-boundary reference" claim is unmeasured. Both belong in the reviewer's lap — the bet survives either correction (warm-start patching at 3 of every 4 sub-segments is still a real lever at this seq_len).

## r1 — 2026-06-11 — verdict: revise
- **Segment is undefined at our tier.** tiny1m3m trains seq_len=2048 with batch=2 over ~733 steps. XL's whole point is to cache across *segments shorter than what fits in one forward pass*. The proposal doesn't say whether "segment" means (a) artificially split 2048→N chunks then cache, (b) cache across batch boundaries (breaks shuffling), or (c) something else. Without that, there's no actual A/B to run. Pick one and write it.
- **Positional scheme collides with load-bearing FIRE/RoPE.** Our baseline runs RoPE base=250k + SWA(window=384), and 009 FIRE-PE is the biggest standalone win on record (Δ -0.064). XL's published recipe uses Shaw-style relative biases that replace absolute/rotary positions — "concatenate cached states into K/V path with relative positions preserving order" hand-waves the conflict. State: do you drop RoPE for XL-rel-bias (which loses the FIRE/RoPE wins from the ctrl), keep RoPE on the in-segment K/V and only attach relative bias to the cache, or stack on top? Each is a different bet with a different outcome.
- **Crisp expectation missing.** "asks whether a cheap memory span is more useful than a single fixed window" is the framing, not the bet. Write the one-line "we expect X because Y" — e.g. "with SWA(384) on, attaching an N-token detached cache moves val loss by Δ because the 384-step horizon is the bottleneck at our seq_len=2048". Quantify N and which window of the SWA family it sits on top of.
- **Tier-fit risk same family as closed 008/012.** Long-context memory mechanisms keep failing at this tier: 008-deltanet `taste-reject: off-niche on tier`, 012 re-pitch `miner concedes mechanism doesn't fire at this scale`, 004-retnet `null at tiny1m3m`. At 3M total tokens with seq_len=2048, most documents already fit inside one segment; cross-segment dependency density is low. To clear taste this round, name the in-niche regime where XL fires *at 733 steps × 3M tokens × seq_len=2048* and why it's not a clone of those rejected bets — a mechanistic argument, not "the paper says".
- **Information value of a null is conditional on the above.** A clean null is only useful once the segment definition and positional composition are pinned — otherwise it's untestable, not informative. Pin them and a null becomes the "cheap memory doesn't fire at 3M tokens" result the queue is missing.
