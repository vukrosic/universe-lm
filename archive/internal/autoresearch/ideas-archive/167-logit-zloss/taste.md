# Taste log — 167 logit-zloss

## r1 — 2026-06-14 — verdict: accept
- **Crisp bet acknowledged.** The miner states the expected outcome: val_loss Δ inside ±0.04 noise band (likely null) because at 0.94M/12L/3M tokens the model lacks the capacity to drive logits to the exploding regime where z-loss fires. Honest framing — likely null but informative either way.
- **Portfolio fit is genuinely orthogonal.** The closed loss-shape axes (066-070: label-smoothing, confidence-penalty, unlikelihood, focal-loss, mtp-head) all target the *prediction/target* side. Z-loss targets *logit magnitude* — a structurally different axis that is currently OPEN in our closed-axes inventory. Filling it is meaningful, not crowding.
- **Information value is high either way.** Win: logit magnitude IS the binding constraint at 0.94M, z-loss is a cheap stabilizer. Null: logit explosion is not the binding constraint at our tier (consistent with the tier-mismatch family — 110-weight-ema, 122-tiger, 124-radam, 134-mega-ema — but on a different axis, logit-magnitude, not optimizer-horizon). Both outcomes are informative and the test is cheap.
- **Niche fit is clean.** Mechanism (not HP), zero-init-able (`zloss_weight=0.0` ⇒ bit-identical at step 0), ~12 LoC, +1 hyperparameter (typical `1e-4` per PaLM). The bit-identity claim is verifiable: implementer should confirm `z = torch.logsumexp(logits, dim=-1).pow(2).mean()` matches expectation, and that with `zloss_weight=0` the total loss is bit-identical to baseline across all 92 steps.
- **Transfer risk honestly stated at MED.** Mechanism is well-validated at ≥100B (PaLM 540B, LLaMA 2, OLMo 2, Gemma) but the failure mode it targets (logit explosion in late training) is largely absent at 0.94M. This is NOT a guaranteed null like 070-mtp-head (paper's own Fig 3 shows MTP hurts at <3B) — the paper does not say z-loss hurts at small scale, only that it stabilizes at large scale. A clean null at tiny1m3m closes the *logit-magnitude* axis at this tier without invalidating the lever for a future larger tier.
- **Step-0 identity is the load-bearing correctness check.** PaLM's recipe is `zloss_weight=1e-4` and the penalty magnitude at typical logits (`log(Z) ≈ 6–11`) is roughly `36–121`, so a 1e-4 contribution is ~`0.004–0.012` — within the noise band. That makes the bet *quantitatively* small at our tier even when on, which is consistent with the likely-null expectation but not a reason to skip the test.
- **Action for the definition gate:** spec must lock in (a) the exact `torch.logsumexp(logits, dim=-1).pow(2).mean()` formulation (NOT `sum_v logits_v`), (b) a bit-identity self-check across all 92 steps, and (c) a single nonzero treatment value (recommend `1e-4` per PaLM, with optional sweep to `3e-4` if the spec is tight enough). The `zloss_weight=0.0` default keeps the baseline bit-identical when off — exactly what the niche demands.
- **In short:** the bet is honest, the axis is open, the lever is real, the test is cheap, the null is informative. Accept.
