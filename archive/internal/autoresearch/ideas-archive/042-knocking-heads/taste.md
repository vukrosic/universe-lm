## r1 — 2026-06-11 — verdict: revise
- **Head-count tier mismatch is the binding concern.** Tiny1m3m uses `n_heads=4` (configs/llm_config.py); the paper's smallest dense ablation is 0.61B — ~650× our scale and presumably H≥16. The `Q/K/V feature-channel cross-head projection` is mechanically a per-channel mix matrix whose *expressive headroom scales with H*. At H=4 the mix has 3 off-diagonal floats of freedom per channel — a regime the paper never tested. The miner's `transfer-risk: low` claim is asserted from billion-scale data, not argued from mechanism. **Re-pitch must give a mechanistic argument for why H=4 with diagonal-init cross-head mixing fires at our depth/width**, not a transfer-up appeal to 6.1B MoE results.
- **Sibling crowding with 041 talking-heads (also `needs-taste`).** Both ask the same family question — *should heads share, and how?* — distinguished only by *where* the mix is inserted: 041 mixes attention probabilities (post-softmax), 042 mixes Q/K/V features (pre-attention). Running both back-to-back wastes two slots on the same bet unless the pitch explicitly partitions the question. **Re-pitch must contrast 042 vs 041 in one sharp sentence**: what does feature-channel mixing test that probability mixing does not, and why is *this* the better head-mixing test at H=4? Bonus: argue why a tournament (041 + 042) is preferable to a single representative — but only if you can name a specific outcome that distinguishes them.
- **No crisp Δ bet.** "Tests whether heads should collaborate before concatenation" is information-value framing, not a sharp prediction. Per the taste prompt: a bet has the form *"we expect Δ -0.0X because Y mechanism"*. Pick a sign and magnitude at tiny1m3m **with H=4 specifically**, and pick a pass/fail bar that makes even the null force a real update (e.g. "null at bar -0.005 → cross-head feature sharing requires H≥12 to register at our token budget" — that's a useful kill).
- **Null-informativeness is uncertain.** At H=4 a null could mean (a) head-mixing is fundamentally irrelevant at any scale, (b) the mechanism needs >4 heads to express, or (c) the paper's gain is a stability effect that only manifests over thousands of steps (paper headlines "reduced training spikes" — a spike-suppression effect over 1T tokens probably won't fire in our ~92-step run). The current pitch treats all three as one undifferentiated "null". Re-pitch must say which interpretation a null at -0.005 vs -0.02 selects.
- **Identity-init claim is stated but not verified.** "Diagonal initialization" is good — re-pitch should confirm step-0 output exactly matches baseline (the diagonal must be 1.0, not 1/H or 1/√H scaled), so a null cannot be blamed on a wiring artifact.

**Closing the gap (concrete re-pitch checklist):**
1. Mechanistic argument for why feature-channel mixing fires at H=4, not why it scales to H=32.
2. One-sentence contrast vs 041 talking-heads — what new degree of freedom does feature mixing add over probability mixing?
3. Sharp Δ prediction at tiny1m3m with a pass/fail bar that partitions null interpretations.
4. Identity-init confirmation (diagonal = 1.0, step-0 = baseline byte-for-byte).
5. Address whether "reduced training spikes" is observable in a 92-step run, or if that aspect of the paper's headline is out-of-scope at our token budget.
