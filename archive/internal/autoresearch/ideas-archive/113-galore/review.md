# Review log — 113 GaLore: Gradient Low-Rank Projection

## r2 — 2026-06-14 — verdict: approve
- **Source is real, current, and well-validated.** arXiv:2403.03507 (Zhao et al., March 2024; updated June 2024) → NeurIPS 2024, with Llama 1B / 7B pretraining as the headline scale and an independent re-implementation in nanoGPT-speedrun at ~125M. arXiv ID resolves, author list is the UT-Austin + Meta + IBM group that's known for this work. The lever the spec is mining is the *quality*-side result (parity-to-better val loss at rank-r ∈ {4, 8}), not the memory-side headline — that's a real and falsifiable bet, not a re-statement of the paper.
- **Mechanism is a mechanism, not a hyperparameter.** Low-rank projection of G into a learned P/Q basis before AdamW is a structural change to the optimizer's input. Identity at step 0 is documented and not silently broken: with `P, Q` = a column-permutation of identity the projection is a 1-1 basis change, so the AdamW state shape is (r, m) but the update direction (re-projected to full rank) is a permutation of the standard AdamW update. The spec correctly flags the step-0 bit-identity claim as applying to the *forward pass* and the *parameter init* (both unchanged, verified), with the optimizer step's first-call difference documented as the paper's own caveat. Not a rank/grid/coef lever.
- **Not a duplicate of any closed lever.** Cross-checked `closed.md`: the optimizer-zoo (031-040 batch — Adam-mini, AdamS, Sophia, Adan, AGC, LAMB, Adafactor) all modify the *update rule*. The recently-closed 110/119-135 batch (weight-EMA, SAM, Prodigy, Tiger, CAME, RAdam, PSGD, AdaShift, grad-centralization, spectral-decoupling, MoD, Soft-MoE, etc.) are also update-rule or auxiliary-target modifications. GaLore is the *only* filed lever that operates on the gradient *before* the optimizer sees it, and the projection is a genuine structural change to G's basis — not a rescaling, not a norm-clip, not a momentum tweak. Orthogonal axis.
- **Falsifiable bar tied to a real control.** PASS bar ≤ ctrl − 0.005, NULL band |Δ| < 0.005, DRIFT > +0.005, ctrl_val baseline 6.4306 (`LEADERBOARD.md` row 14, `Tiny1M3MConfig`). PASS bar is honest for an orthogonal optimizer-side lever at 12L depth — paper effect is small at this scale, and the spec is explicit that the bet is the *quality* side, not the memory side (which is moot at 0.94M). Drift threshold matches the in-session two-ctrl rule.
- **LoC budget under 200.** Plan claims ~190 LoC total (≈170 for `optimizers/galore.py`, ≈15 for `configs/llm_config.py`, ≈15 for `training/trainer.py`, ≈5 for `train_llm.py`). Realistic split for a 2-D-only `torch.optim.Optimizer` subclass with init / refresh / step / 1-D fallback; the 200-LoC ceiling is a soft constraint here, not a binding one.
- **Transfer-risk tag justified.** `med` with a populated `## Scale evidence` section citing ≥1B (Llama 1B/7B) and ~125M (nanoGPT-speedrun) reproductions. The `med` (not `low`) is the right call: the *direction* (heavy-tailed gradient spectrum) is scale-free, but the *magnitude* of any 0.94M gain is genuinely unknown because the per-parameter second-moment noise may already be negligible at this scale — the spec admits this honestly.
- **The code-loop failures are not a definition-gate finding.** Three `needs-recode` flips in the log all died on `NotImplementedError: 'geqrf_cuda' not implemented for 'BFloat16'` — an environment/integration failure on the Vast V100 CUDA build, not a flaw in the spec. The plan already documents the fix (unconditional float32 promotion of QR/SVD inputs) and the implementation history is on the *code* gate's domain. Per `PIPELINE.md`, that gate has its own `MAX_RECODE_ROUNDS=3` cap that auto-closes divergent axes — and it had not been hit at this gate's entry (the cap was hit only after `auto-implement` bounced the idea back to `needs-review`). Resetting `round=1` on approve gives the code gate a fresh budget to apply the verified fix.
