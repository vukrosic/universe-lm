## r1 — 2026-06-09 — verdict: accept
- **Mechanism**: surgical. Two `nn.LayerNorm(d_head)` modules applied along the `d_head` axis of Q and K *after* the q/k_proj reshape into `(B,H,T,d_head)` and *before* the attention dot product. Because each Q/K vector is unit-RMS along `d_head`, the per-head attention logit `Q·K/√d_head` is bounded in magnitude by `√d_head`, preventing logit explosion that softens the softmax. < 15 LoC, drop-in to `MHA.forward`. Init γ=1, β=0 → step-0 is bit-identical to baseline.
- **Transferability**: the lever is *per-head logit bounding* — strictly local. The closed norm-zoo (pnorm/manhattan/center/squash/clip/channelscale) all kept LN's reduce-then-normalize template at the *residual stream* and showed NULL. 016 is a categorically different site (per-head Q/K) and a different function (logit bounding, not activation re-centering). ViT/Chameleon wins are at 20+ layers, but at 6 layers the per-head logit drift is small — leverage is bounded.
- **Bet** (crisp): "we expect a small win because the residual stream's per-head Q/K magnitudes can grow with depth even at 6 layers (pre-norm drift is well-known), and bounding them at the head-dim level keeps the softmax sharp without adding a residual-stream norm." Single global boolean.
- **Null teaching**: a clean null at 6 layers closes a specific question — "QK-Norm's benefit is concentrated in deeper stacks (≥12 layers); the per-head logit drift at 6 layers is below threshold." That's worth knowing because the closed norm-zoo nulls couldn't partition this from a residual-stream question.
- **Portfolio**: 015 (Muon variant) and 018 (AdEMAMix) are in the optimizer family. 016 is attention-stability — different family, no crowding. 017 (sub-LN) is also depth-stability, but 016 and 017 are orthogonal mechanisms (per-head logit bounding vs per-sublayer output bounding), so testing both in the same batch is fine — they partition the depth-stability axis cleanly.
- **Caveat for the reviewer**: leverage at 6 layers is bounded — the bet is at the low end of what QK-Norm was shown to do. Definition gate should set a reasonable PASS bar (~-0.005 to -0.01) and treat the result as "depth-stability upper bound" rather than a headline win. Wire as a single `use_qk_norm` flag; the miner already pinned the placement site and the LN identity-init.
