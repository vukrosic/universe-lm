- **Mechanism**: clean. Per-tensor RMS rescale `c·sqrt(max(d_in, d_out))·O` is a geometric-calibration lever on top of the orthogonalized Muon update — it normalizes the *element-wise* step magnitude across matrix shapes, so every 2D weight (1:1 attention heads, 1:4 FFN up) gets approximately unit-RMS updates. Distinct from sign-mask (cautious), eigenbasis precond (SOAP), iterate averaging (schedule-free), and routing (decoupled-QKV). The post-mul is a single new scalar; < 10 LoC.
- **Transferability**: shape mismatch is real at our scale (6 layers × {Q/K/V heads 1:1, FFN up 1:4, FFN down 4:1}), so the lever is *applicable* — but the paper's win is reported at 16B+ params, and at tiny1m3m the mismatch is geometrically present but the *update budget* is short, so the implicit-LR-rebalance effect may not accumulate. Step-0 is bit-identical (ortho of zero gradient = zero), so identity-init is preserved.
- **Bet** (crisp): "we expect a measurable win because plain Muon gives every tensor a unit-spectral update but wildly different RMS, so the wide FFN matrices get larger element-wise steps than the attention heads — `c·sqrt(max(d_in,d_out))` re-balances the step magnitude across shapes." Single knob `c ≈ 0.2`.
