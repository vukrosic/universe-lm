## r1 — 2026-06-09 — verdict: accept
- **Mechanism**: sub-LN replaces the standard pre-norm sublayer `y = x + Sublayer(LN_pre(x))` with the sandwiched form `y = x + LN_post(Sublayer(LN_pre(x)))`. `LN_post` is a fresh `nn.LayerNorm(d_model)` per sublayer, init γ=1, β=0, so at step 0 the two LNs collapse algebraically to the identity and the residual stream is bit-identical to the pre-norm baseline. One new LN per attention block and one per FFN block, wired into `TransformerBlock.forward` around the existing `self.attn` / `self.mlp` calls. < 20 LoC net new.
- **Transferability**: the lever is the *interior* of the pre-norm / post-norm axis — pre-norm (closed: baseline) and post-norm (closed) are the corners, sub-LN is the *gradient-preserving* midpoint that bounds each sublayer's contribution to unit-RMS at the residual stream. At 6 layers the per-sublayer drift is small, so leverage is bounded; DeepNet ablations show the win at 100+ layers. Init is clean, so the A/B isolates the placement change.
- **Bet** (crisp): "we expect a small win (or null) at 6 layers because pre-norm drift grows the residual stream's scale across depth, and `LN_post` constrains each sublayer's contribution locally — fighting the drift at the source instead of relying on the closed post-norm's global re-centering." Single boolean on `TransformerBlock`.
- **Null teaching**: a clean null at 6 layers is informative — "sub-LN's drift-fighting only fires in deep stacks (≥24 layers); pre-norm's drift is sub-threshold at 6 layers." This partitions the depth-stability axis from 016-qk-norm: 016 tests per-head logit bounding (attention site), 017 tests per-sublayer output bounding (residual site).
- **Portfolio**: 016 (QK-Norm) is also a depth-stability lever, but 016 is at the *attention score* site and 017 is at the *residual stream* site — orthogonal mechanisms, both partition the same question (does depth-stability matter at 6 layers?) into separate sites. Testing both in one batch is fine because a clean double-null is itself informative ("both depth-stability levers are below threshold at 6 layers"). The closed norm-zoo is residual-stream re-centering; 017 is residual-stream *re-bounding* — different function.
- **Caveat for the reviewer**: leverage at 6 layers is bounded; treat the result as a depth-stability probe, not a headline bet. Definition gate should set a low PASS bar (~-0.005) since the expected effect is small; the null is the more informative outcome. Wire as a single `sub_ln` flag on `TransformerBlock`; miner already pinned γ=1, β=0 and the placement.
