# Taste log — 070 multi-token prediction head

## r1 — 2026-06-11 — verdict: reject
- **Mechanism**: clear and small. K-1 auxiliary unembedding heads on the shared trunk predict tokens t+2 … t+K; main loss is `CE(t+1) + Σ λ_k · CE(t+k)`. At λ_k=0 init the run is bit-identical to NTP, but the heads add params for the whole run.
- **Scale axis — the bet can't fire at tiny1m3m, and paper's own data says so.** Gloeckle et al. (arXiv:2404.19737) Section 3 / Figure 3 explicitly report that **MTP HURTS at small scales** (~300M, 1B is a wash-to-worse vs NTP) and benefit only emerges past ~3B; the headline gains are reported at 6.7B/13B. tiny1m3m (0.94M params · 3M tokens) is **~13,000× smaller than the paper's validation scale** and ~300× smaller than where MTP is *worst* relative to NTP. The paper's mechanistic claim (richer supervision regularizes overparam'd large models) is the opposite of what tiny1m3m is — capacity-limited, embedding-dominated, undertrained by Chinchilla.
- **`transfer-risk: low` is mis-tagged.** The miner's frontmatter says low because "method validated on large LMs," which is exactly backward — *validation at 13B with documented small-scale regression* is the **high** transfer-risk profile, not low. Compare to closed twin **018-ademamix** (taste-reject: "bet cannot fire at tiny1m3m … paper explicitly states the gap widens with training length") — same failure mode, EMA-half-life version. Same call here on the scale axis.
- **Param-budget injection.** Each extra head is roughly `d_model × vocab_size` parameters. At tiny1m3m the LM head + embeddings already dominate the 0.94M budget; even one extra head is a sizeable fraction of total params, and 3 heads (the paper's main config) would shift the param budget materially against a same-arch NTP control. Either the A/B is unfair-by-param (treatment has more capacity) or unfair-by-tied-head (mechanism degraded). No clean A/B exists at this scale.
- **Information value: zero either way.** A null reconfirms the paper's own published small-scale regression — adds nothing to the literature or our recipe. A win would be surprising precisely because it contradicts the paper's scaling data, but with one seed and a param-imbalanced A/B it would not be trustable. The "what does this teach us whether it wins or loses" bar (idea-taste.md §2) is hard-fail.
- **Crisp bet?** "Multi-token heads test whether tiny1m3m benefits from richer supervision" — no signed prediction, no mechanistic hook to *small-scale*, no expected Δ. Vibe, not a bet (idea-taste.md §2).
- **Crowded family.** DeepSeek V3 ships MTP; many follow-ups. Adopted, not novel.
- **Niche-fit rule** (idea-taste.md §2): *"An idea that only pays off at larger scale … has no taste here regardless of how good the paper is."* MTP is the canonical example — paper's own figure 3 says so. **Reject.**

**Suggestion for miner (not part of verdict):** MTP is a legitimate large-scale lever; file it on the 135M+/data-mix backlog (queue.md "Scale-tier backlog"), not as a tiny1m3m candidate. Do not re-pitch at this tier.
