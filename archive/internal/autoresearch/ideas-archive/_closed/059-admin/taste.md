- Info value is fake: the "Why it's worth a slot" claim ("a null would point away from init geometry as the main issue") cannot be earned by *this* idea — at 6L a null is consistent with both (a) init geometry not mattering and (b) Admin's depth correction degenerating to ~identity. The A/B can't distinguish those, so the null isn't informative — it's the predicted outcome of the off-niche test.
- Portfolio collision: queue already carries the depth-init family at saturation — 055-deepnorm (depth-derived residual scaling), 056-branchnorm, 058-fixup (residual learning w/o norm, near-identity start), 060-ngpt. Five back-to-back ablations of the same mechanism family violate the diversify-the-portfolio rule even if each is individually defensible; Admin is the weakest of the five for tiny1m3m because its paper scale is purely NMT and its correction is the most depth-coupled.
- Niche fit: not a clean "mechanism shows its effect at tiny1m3m" candidate. The paper is NMT, the published gains (≤2.5 BLEU) are at 60 layers, and there is no LM-side replication at small depth in the source. Reject rather than revise: there is no re-pitch that keeps Admin's identity (the depth-aware init recipe) while making the 6L bet sharp.
