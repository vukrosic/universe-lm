## r1 — 2026-06-09 — verdict: reject
- **Mechanism**: clear. A second EMA `m2_t` with slow decay β3 ≈ 0.9999 (half-life ≈ 7k steps) is added to standard AdamW's `(m1, v)`, and the update mixes `m1 + α·m2` before applying the `1/(√v+eps)` step. α warms 0 → α_final (paper: 5-8) over T_α. Step 0 is bit-identical to AdamW (m2=0, α=0). ~+33% optimizer-state memory.
- **Transferability**: **the bet can't fire at tiny1m3m.** The slow EMA has half-life ≈ 7k steps. tiny1m3m is 3M tokens; with batch ≈ 16 × seq 2048 = 32768 tok/step, that's ~92 steps total. At 92 steps, `β3^92 ≈ 0.9999^92 ≈ 0.991` — the slow EMA is still 99% weighted toward its init (zero) and has accumulated ≈ 1% of one effective gradient observation. **The slow EMA is essentially inactive for the entire run.** The paper explicitly states the gap *widens* with training length, so the lever only fires at run horizons ≥ 100k steps. Tiny1m3m is two-to-three orders of magnitude below the paper's regime.
- **Bet** (crisp, but unfalsifiable at our scale): "we expect a win because mixing old+new gradient direction (temporal averaging window) helps late in training when recent gradients are noisy." This is the *paper's* claim at paper scale. At our scale the temporal window hasn't opened yet, so we cannot test the claim. The mechanism is genuinely orthogonal to existing optimizer variants (cautious-muon, cautious-adamw, SOAP, schedule-free, decoupled-QKV) — but orthogonality is a review-board argument when the lever can fire. Here, it cannot.
- **Null teaching**: a null at tiny1m3m is expected and uninformative — it just confirms "the slow EMA is inactive at this run length," which we can already compute from the EMA math. A win at tiny1m3m would be surprising (it'd imply α is helping through the fast EMA only, which is structurally AdamW). Neither outcome teaches us anything about the *paper's* mechanism. The prompt: *"A clean null result must still be worth logging. Ideas whose only outcome is 'meh, inconclusive' fail."* This is exactly that.
- **Portfolio**: 6+ recent optimizer NULLs (001, 002, 003, 005, 006) plus 011 planning, plus 015 just accepted. 018 would be the 8th. Even setting aside the 5-optimizer rule, **the 018 result cannot move the bar** at our scale.
- **Niche-fit rule**: the prompt's bar is explicit — *"An idea that only pays off at larger scale, or needs data/infra we don't have, has no taste here regardless of how good the paper is."* 018 fails this rule on the larger-scale axis. Reject.
