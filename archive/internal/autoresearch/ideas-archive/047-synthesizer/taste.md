## r1 — 2026-06-11 — verdict: revise

- **Spec the variant.** Tay et al. 2020 defines *four* synthesizers: Dense (MLP over hidden state), Random (no learnable params, position-only), Factorized Dense (low-rank), and Mixture (Vanilla + Dense). They have different priors — Random is known-bad on LM (paper's Table 2), Dense is the only one that roughly matches vanilla on language modeling. The current `idea.md` says "learned or random score generators" without picking one. **Pick Dense Synthesizer** (small two-layer MLP h_i → score_i, position-independent); that is the one variant with any non-zero prior of helping, and the variant the paper itself flags as worth re-testing.
- **Sharpen the bet to a number.** "Clean null" is not a bet. The miner needs to commit to: (a) the predicted mass-share the gate learns to use (e.g., "synthetic branch settles at ≤0.1 of total attention mass, dot-product branch carries ≥0.9" — i.e., model rejects the synthetic path), AND (b) the val-loss Δ bound that proves the synthetic path is engaged (e.g., "if the gate mean is >0.3 and trt val is within noise of the fire-ctrl, that's an uninformative win"). Without (a) and (b) the result reads as "gating learned a non-trivial weight" and we don't know if that matters.
- **Transfer-risk: high needs a mechanistic argument, not a hedge.** "Strongest evidence is broad task coverage rather than large-scale LM pretraining" is the miner admitting the paper doesn't actually show this on pretraining at the scale we run at. A high-transfer lever that has not been demonstrated on LM pretraining >100M is exactly the recipe for a known-null slot. The miner should either (i) explain the *mechanism* by which a content-free attention pattern could plausibly help at 135M (e.g., "synthetic scoring provides a learned fixed prior that improves length-generalization on small heads where QK is noisy") and add it to `## Scale evidence`, or (ii) down-grade `transfer-risk` to `medium` with a concrete prior from the paper's closest LM experiment.
- **"Hybrid heads" already closed on this repo's axes.** `autoresearch/closed.md` lists `NSA / diff-attn / hybrid heads` as a closed axis. The zero-gated synthetic branch IS a hybrid head. Not a literal duplicate of NSA/diff-attn, but in the same family the miner/reviewer already swept at screen20m. The pitch has to explain why the result wouldn't be "we already swept hybrid-heads and they lost" — at minimum by citing the screen20m null if one exists, or by saying this is the first non-vanilla hybrid that has a non-zero prior.
