## r1 — 2026-06-09 — verdict: accept
- **Mechanism**: FoX multiplies the attention matrix by a causal decay kernel `D_h[i,j] = exp(cumsum(log f)[i] - cumsum(log f)[j])`, where `f_h,t = sigmoid(W_f^h · x_t + b_f^h)` is a per-head, per-token learned forget gate in (0,1). The decay is *multiplicative on probabilities* (after the softmax), then row-renormalized. A per-head `nn.Linear(d_model, H)` produces the gate logits, plus a cumsum of `log f` along the time axis to build the kernel in O(T) memory. < 90 LoC, ~5% attention forward overhead at 6 layers × T=2048.
- **Transferability**: strictly orthogonal to every closed attention lever. Closed axes: NSA/diff-attn modify the *score function*; SWA/dilated attention modify the *mask*; RoPE/NoPE modify *position encoding*; logit softcap clamps the *logit range*; the closed delta-net / linear-attention family (008, 012) replaces softmax with linear attention. **None of these introduce a content-conditional, per-head, per-token learned decay multiplier on A.*** FoX is a *conservative extension of softmax attention* — the softmax stays, the projection stays, the V path is unchanged. Distinct from all of the above.
- **Identity-init**: clean. `b_f^h = +5` (sigmoid → 0.993, log f ≈ -0.007) and `W_f^h = 0` → `D_h[i,j] ≈ 1` everywhere at step 0. Attention is numerically identical to baseline at initialization; the model has to *learn* to forget from scratch. This is a real identity, not a "close to identity" approximation.
- **Bet** (crisp): "we expect FoX to win on top of 009 FIRE because FIRE is an **additive position bias on attention *logits*** (changes *which* key wins) and FoX is a **multiplicative decay on attention *probabilities*** (changes *how much mass* even the winners keep). The two axes are orthogonal. If FoX wins, the missing axis is *mass control*; if it nulls, FIRE's additive bias already saturates the relative-position benefit at our scale." Builds on the 009 WIN, doesn't replicate it.
- **Null teaching**: a clean null partitions a specific question — "FIRE's additive bias is sufficient for relative-position control at tiny1m3m; the multiplicative mass-control axis is sub-threshold at our depth/length." Informative either way.
- **Portfolio**: 015 (Muon), 016 (QK-Norm), 017 (sub-LN), 019 (DyT) are in the active batch. 020 is a *new family* — attention-probability modification, distinct from all the above. No crowding. Builds cleanly on 009's WIN without competing for the same axis.
