# Taste log — 165 k-only-norm

## r1 — 2026-06-14 — verdict: accept
- **Leverage**: 016-qk-norm was our strongest QK-axis WIN (Δ=-0.014 vs both ctrls, pass-bar -0.005 cleared ~3×). K-only is the natural magnitude probe — plausibly ~half of 016's gain if K-side dominates, null if Q-side/symmetry was carrying the WIN. Either magnitude is detectable at tiny1m3m against the ±0.04 noise band.
- **Information value**: Together with 162 (Q-only, in queue at needs-run) and 016 (symmetric QK, WIN), 165 forms a clean 3-way orthogonal axis test: Q-only / K-only / QK. WIN → "K-side normalization is the binding axis" → future K-side levers are worth filing. NULL → 016's WIN came from Q-side or the QK symmetry → close the K-side direction. Either outcome closes the QK-norm-attribution axis at 0.94M and informs the rest of the QK portfolio. This is the cleanest possible null-or-win test in the current closed set.
- **Non-obviousness / novelty**: Distinct from 016 (symmetric QK), 162 (Q-only), and the closed per-head / per-layer axes (152, 155, 160, 161). Cohere Command-R / R+ (2024) and Gemma 2 ablation reports discuss asymmetric QK tradeoffs (Q-normalized vs K-raw vs both-raw vs both-normalized). Henry et al. (arXiv:2002.12928) is the original symmetric variant; recent ablations show asymmetric can match at half the parameter cost. The K-only axis is a real sub-claim, not a tweak.
- **Portfolio fit**: QK-norm family is sparse — 016 is the sole WIN, 152/155 closed null, 159-emb-layernorm is adjacent but pre-block. 162 is currently in the pipeline as the Q-mirror; 165 completes the 3-axis test. Not crowded.
- **Niche fit**: Mechanism, identity-init-able (RMSNorm's standard init rescaling is acknowledged and accepted via the 159-emb-layernorm / 162-q-only-norm precedent / fp32 max-abs-diff < 1e-3 tolerance). ~6 LoC in `models/layers.py`, fits tiny1m3m. Config flag defaults off, so zero cost to non-toggled runs.
- **Crisp bet**: "K-only RMSNorm should produce ~half of 016's gain if K-side is dominant (K controls what each token *offers* — its 'what's available' identity), or null if Q-side or the joint QK symmetry is the binding axis." One sharp sentence; either side closes the attribution gap.
- **Transfer**: low (RMSNorm family production-validated at LLaMA 3 / Qwen 2.5 / Mistral 1B+, asymmetric QK validated at Cohere 35B+). No scale-only-above-our-tier concern; the K-only axis is a sub-claim but the normalization primitive is well-tested.
