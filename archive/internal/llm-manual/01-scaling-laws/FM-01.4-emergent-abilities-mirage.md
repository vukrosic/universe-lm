# FM-01.4 — "Emergent abilities" are largely a metric artifact, not a phase change

**Rule.** Sudden, discontinuous capability jumps with scale ("emergent abilities") are mostly
produced by **nonlinear/thresholded evaluation metrics** (e.g. exact-match accuracy) plus
**too few test samples on small models**. Under a continuous metric, the same capability
improves *smoothly and predictably* with scale. Do not plan around — or fear — unpredictable
phase transitions in your own training.

**Confidence.** **[C] Contested.** The metric-artifact mechanism is demonstrated and widely
accepted as *a* major cause; whether it explains *all* emergence is actively debated.

**Scope.** Schaeffer et al. 2023, analysis of fixed model outputs across the GPT-3 family and
BIG-Bench; argument is about *measurement*, not a claim that capabilities never improve.

## Evidence

Schaeffer, Miranda, Koyejo 2023, "Are Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models a Mirage?"
(NeurIPS 2023):

- A nonlinear metric (accuracy: per-token error compounds *geometrically* over the answer
  length) manufactures an apparent sharp jump; swapping to a near-linear metric (token edit
  distance) on the *same outputs* reveals a smooth curve.
- Small test sets make small models look like they score *zero* (they're just below the
  measurement floor), exaggerating the apparent onset.
- Quant: **>92% of BIG-Bench "emergent" task instances** appear only under nonlinear/
  discontinuous metrics.

**The debate (why [C]):** critics (2025 survey; arithmetic-task analyses) argue some jumps
persist under better metrics, and that the substitute metrics (e.g. token edit distance on
arithmetic) can *hide* genuine capability gaps — a wrong sum gets only a small edit penalty.
So: metric choice explains *much* of emergence, not provably all.

## Why it matters for a practitioner

- **Predictability.** Smooth underlying curves mean you *can* forecast a larger model's
  capability from smaller ones with the right (continuous) metric — don't assume a magic
  threshold will appear or rescue you.
- **Evaluation hygiene.** Prefer continuous, per-token or graded metrics; report enough test
  samples that small models clear the measurement floor. This directly informs how *we* judge
  tiny-model results.

## Reproduced by us?

**Methodologically aligned, not formally tested.** The ledger already enforces the dual of
this rule: continuous loss/Δ over thresholded pass/fail, and explicit measurement-floor
discipline (noise floor `L001`). The deep connection: *our entire premise — that 1–3M-param
experiments inform real models (G3) — depends on capability curves being smooth and
predictable across scale.* If emergence were a true unpredictable phase change, tiny-scale
science would be near-worthless. This rule is part of why the bet is reasonable.

## Falsifier

A capability that, under a continuous metric with adequate test statistics, *still* shows a
genuinely discontinuous jump at a specific scale (not explainable by metric or sample size)
would bound the "mirage" claim and restore real emergence for that capability.
