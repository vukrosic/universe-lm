# FM-02.1 — Quality filtering beats raw scale at equal compute

**Rule.** At a fixed compute/token budget, a model trained on **aggressively quality-filtered**
web text outperforms one trained on more, dirtier text. Quality is a multiplier on compute;
spend the engineering on the filtering pipeline.

**Confidence.** **[E] Established.** Demonstrated with public ablations (FineWeb / FineWeb-Edu)
and corroborated across labs (C4, Gopher, RefinedWeb, Dolma).

**Scope.** Web-scale pretraining of dense LLMs. "Quality" = removing boilerplate, low-info,
and off-distribution text via heuristics + (increasingly) learned classifiers. The *best*
filter is somewhat target-dependent.

## Evidence / details

- **FineWeb** (15T tokens from 96 Common Crawl dumps) beat other open corpora at equal scale by
  documenting and ablating every filtering/dedup choice — **70+ ablation models** released, the
  first fully-transparent demonstration that pipeline choices, not just size, drive quality.
- **FineWeb-Edu** (1.3T tokens, classifier-filtered for *educational* content) dramatically
  beat unfiltered FineWeb on MMLU and ARC — a smaller, *more-filtered* set won on knowledge/
  reasoning benchmarks. Targeted quality filtering > raw volume.
- Lineage: C4 (boilerplate + langID + dedup → T5), Gopher quality filters, RefinedWeb (showed
  well-filtered web *alone* can rival curated multi-source), Dolma (200TB → ~3T clean tokens).
- Emerging frontier: formalizing quality *inside* scaling laws (most scaling laws are quality-
  agnostic; recent work adds a quality term) — matters most in data-limited specialized domains.

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

This is where a compute-constrained team gets the most leverage. You usually can't add compute,
but you *can* filter harder — and FineWeb-Edu shows that can be worth more than scaling tokens.
For a city/company training its own model on a limited domain corpus, the filtering pipeline is
the highest-ROI engineering after compute allocation.

## Reproduced by us?

**No — data is held fixed by design.** The ledger isolates architecture, so we deliberately do
*not* vary data quality; every structural result is conditional on our fixed corpus. We cite
this; we don't measure it. (A clean future bridge: does the best architecture change with data
quality? Currently unmeasured.)

## Falsifier

An in-scope, equal-compute comparison where adding lower-quality tokens beats quality-filtering
them out would bound the rule (likely only in extreme data-starvation, where FM-01.2 repetition
trades against quality).
