# FM-02.2 — Deduplicate first; near-duplicates waste compute and hurt

**Rule.** Deduplicate the corpus — exact **and** fuzzy/near-duplicate — **before** quality
filtering and mixing. Dedup improves loss even at *fixed* token budget, and uncontrolled
duplication compounds into memorization and degraded generalization as model size grows.

**Confidence.** **[E] Established.** Standard, load-bearing step in every modern pipeline.

**Scope.** Web-scale pretraining. Method: exact-match + MinHash-LSH near-duplicate clustering,
applied globally (cross-document, cross-shard).

## Evidence / details

- **Fixed-budget benefit:** Gopher found rigorous dedup + low-quality removal lowered perplexity
  *even when token count was held constant* — duplicates aren't just redundant, they're harmful.
- **Scale of redundancy:** SlimPajama's global MinHash-LSH removed **~49.6%** of 1.2T tokens →
  a denser 627B-token set. Roughly half of raw web text is near-duplicate mass.
- **Ordering:** dedup **before** quality scoring and domain mixing — otherwise duplicate mass
  skews quality classifiers and mixture proportions.
- **Connection to repetition (FM-01.2):** dedup removes *unintentional* repetition; FM-01.2
  governs *intentional* epoch repetition. Both point the same way — repeated tokens teach less
  and eventually hurt, more so at larger model size.
- **Refinement — "rehydration" (FineWeb2):** instead of hard-deleting duplicates, keep cluster
  size as metadata and *upsample* documents proportionally to duplication rate — turning dedup
  from destructive removal into a sampling signal, recovering value lost to over-aggressive
  deletion.

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

Cheap, mechanical, and one of the highest-ROI steps available — it can ~halve your token count
with *no* quality loss (often a gain), directly stretching a fixed compute budget.

## Reproduced by us?

**No — held fixed.** Our corpus is fixed; we don't vary dedup. Cited as background.

## Falsifier

An in-scope, fixed-budget comparison where a deduplicated corpus trains a *worse* model than
the raw duplicated one would break the rule.
