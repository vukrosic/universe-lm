# FM-06.2 — Facts become *extractable* only if seen in varied phrasings

**Rule.** A fact memorized from a *single* phrasing during pretraining can be **stored but not
extractable** — the model will fail QA about it, and **no amount of later fine-tuning fixes
this (≈0% accuracy).** Knowledge becomes extractable only when pretraining sees it in
**diverse forms** (paraphrase, reordering, translation). So: *rewrite/augment pretraining
data*, and mix QA-style data *into pretraining*, not only after.

**Confidence.** **[F] Frontier.** Allen-Zhu & Li, ICML 2024 (Part 3.1) + Part 3.2; rigorous
and mechanistically explained (linear probing), but single-program / synthetic — independent
replication would upgrade it.

**Scope.** Semi-synthetic biographies, (person, attribute) facts; decoder LLMs. The "fine-
tuning can't rescue it" result is strong within this controlled setting.

## Evidence / details

- **Storage ≠ extraction.** Train on one fixed biography per person → the model memorizes the
  text but answers attribute questions at chance, *even after QA fine-tuning*. Augment each
  biography (paraphrase, shuffle sentences, translate) → near-perfect extraction.
- **Mechanism (linear probing).** *With* augmentation, the model encodes a person's attributes
  *linearly on the name tokens* — knowledge is bound to the entity, retrievable. *Without*, it
  smears attributes across all biography tokens — present but not addressable by the name.
- **Best augmentation:** combining multiple biographies with **permutation** → near-perfect
  across attribute types.
- **Manipulation limits (Part 3.2):** even when a fact is extractable forward, models struggle
  to *manipulate* it (inverse search "who has birthday X?", comparisons) without explicit
  chain-of-thought — knowledge is more directional/brittle than it looks.
- **The timing rule:** inject instruction/QA-style data **during** pretraining, "before it
  becomes too late" — post-hoc fine-tuning cannot create extractability that pretraining didn't.

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

This is the deepest *data-engineering* result in the manual. It says raw scraped text is
**under-augmented**: facts appearing once in one phrasing are wasted capacity. The practical
move — **rewrite pretraining data with a small auxiliary model to add phrasing diversity** — is
now a known lever for making models actually *use* what they ingest. It also explains why
fine-tuning often fails to "teach" facts: it can only surface what pretraining made extractable.

## Reproduced by us?

**No — but mechanistically adjacent to our interests.** This is a *data* result, outside the
ledger's structural-lever scope, and needs synthetic entity data we don't use. Worth knowing
as background: it's a reminder that at our tiny scale on real text, *extractability* is not
something we measure at all — we measure loss. A possible bridge: whether augmentation-style
data diversity changes the *structural* levers' effect sizes (does the best architecture depend
on how augmented the data is?). Speculative; flag before pursuing.

## Falsifier

An in-scope setup where single-phrasing facts become reliably extractable through fine-tuning
alone (no pretraining augmentation), or where augmentation gives no extraction benefit, would
break the rule.
