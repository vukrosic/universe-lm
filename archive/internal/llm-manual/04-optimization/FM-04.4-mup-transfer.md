# FM-04.4 — μP lets you transfer hyperparameters from small models to large

**Rule.** Under the **Maximal Update Parametrization (μP)** — specific width-dependent scalings
of initialization variance and per-layer learning rates — the optimal hyperparameters (peak
LR, init scale, etc.) become **stable across model width.** So you tune on a small model and
**zero-shot transfer** to the large one, paying full-scale tuning cost *once* or never.

**Confidence.** **[C] Contested.** The theory is solid and the headline results are real, but
transfer is **fragile to implementation details** — trainable norm gains, certain optimizers,
per-layer biases can all break it if not μP-aligned. Works when done carefully; bites when not.

**Scope.** Yang et al. 2021 (Tensor Programs V). Transfer is cleanest across **width**; depth
transfer is more delicate. Derived for SGD and Adam.

## Evidence / details

- **Mechanism:** μP keeps the training dynamics (feature-learning regime) consistent as width
  → ∞, so the loss-vs-HP landscape doesn't move with size. Optimal HPs found small stay optimal
  large.
- **Headline results:** tuning a **13M** model beat published **BERT-large (350M)**; tuning a
  **40M** proxy beat published **GPT-3 6.7B**, at ~7% of pretraining cost for tuning.
- **What breaks it:** trainable normalization gains, Lion-style optimizers, per-layer bias
  inclusion, and other deviations from μP-prescribed scaling can disrupt transfer. The Cerebras
  practitioner's guide documents the gotchas; `pip install mup` (microsoft/mup) implements it.
- **Why it matters beyond cost:** μP reduces the *uncertainty* of scaling up — you change size
  without re-entering an unknown HP landscape.

## Reproduced by us?

**Not used; relevant to our central bet.** We don't run μP (single fixed tiny config), but μP
is the field's *proof of concept that small-scale tuning transfers up* — the exact premise
behind the ledger's G3 agenda (do tiny structural findings transfer to ≥135M?). μP transfers
*hyperparameters*; we ask whether *structural levers* transfer. If we ever scale-bridge an
experiment, parametrizing in μP would make the small→large comparison far cleaner — worth a
note when Phase-2 (135M) work begins.

## Falsifier

A correctly-implemented μP setup where the optimal LR (or init) still shifts substantially with
width across the trained range would bound the transfer claim.
