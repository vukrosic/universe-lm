# FM-03.1 — The convergent transformer recipe (and why each piece is there)

**Rule.** For a from-scratch text LLM today, the default architecture is a **decoder-only
transformer, next-token objective**, with **pre-RMSNorm**, **RoPE** positions, **SwiGLU**
FFN, and **Grouped-Query Attention**. Deviate only with a specific, tested reason; the
recipe is a strong prior, not a law of nature.

**Confidence.** **[E] Established.** Five-plus independent frontier labs converged on this
exact bundle, which is about as strong as empirical evidence in this field gets.

**Scope.** Dense decoder-only text LLMs, ~10⁸–10¹² params, standard context lengths. Each
component's *justification* has its own scope (noted below) — and several were established at
scale, so their applicability at **tiny scale (our regime)** is genuinely open.

## The components and their reasons

### Decoder-only, next-token prediction
The backbone is unchanged from GPT-2: token embeddings → N stacked transformer blocks →
next-token softmax. Encoder-decoder and masked-LM objectives lost out for *generative*
scaling; decoder-only with causal attention is the default.

### Pre-norm placement  ·  *reason: trainable depth*
Normalize the **input** of each sub-layer (attention, FFN), inside the residual stream,
rather than the output (post-norm). Pre-norm gives well-behaved gradients at initialization,
which is what makes deep stacks trainable without elaborate warmup tricks. **Caveat:** OLMo-2
deliberately went *back* to post-norm (inside the skip) and trains fine — so pre-norm is the
safe default, not the only option. Placement interacts with init and residual scaling
(connects to DeepNet-style residual scaling, which our ledger `L007` touches).

### RMSNorm (over LayerNorm)  ·  *reason: cheaper, equal quality*
Drop the mean-subtraction and the bias; normalize by root-mean-square only, one learned gain
vector. ~10–50% cheaper in the norm op, half the params, and matches LayerNorm quality. Free
efficiency that compounds at scale.

### RoPE (over absolute/sinusoidal)  ·  *reason: relative + extrapolation*
Rotary Position Embedding rotates Q and K by a position-dependent angle in each layer, so
attention scores depend on *relative* position. Two payoffs: (1) it injects relative position
without a separate bias table; (2) it extends to longer contexts than trained (especially
with interpolation/scaling tricks). This is the field's default — but note it is *not* what
our champion uses: our ledger's best tiny-model lever is a learnable-ALiBi slope + poly
curvature, an additive distance penalty. **RoPE vs ALiBi-family at tiny scale is an open
question we are uniquely positioned to test** (ledger Q1.4).

### SwiGLU FFN (over ReLU/GELU)  ·  *reason: better quality at equal FLOPs*
Gated linear unit with a Swish gate: `FFN(x) = (Swish(xW_gate) ⊙ xW_up) W_down`. Costs a
third projection, so the hidden dim is shrunk to keep FLOPs ≈ constant. Shazeer 2020 showed
gated FFNs consistently beat plain ones; now the default.

### GQA (over Multi-Head Attention)  ·  *reason: inference KV-cache cost*
Share one set of K/V heads across a group of Q heads (between full MHA and single-KV MQA).
**4–8× smaller KV cache** with near-zero quality loss → the main lever for cheaper long-
context inference. Note this is an *inference-cost* optimization, not a quality one; at small
scale or short context its benefit is marginal. (MLA in DeepSeek pushes the same idea
further.)

## Reproduced by us?

**This is the heart of our overlap with the field.** We do not re-derive the recipe; we
*interrogate* it at 1–3M params, where its scale-established justifications may not hold:

- Positional: our champion uses learnable ALiBi, **not** RoPE — direct, ongoing comparison.
- Residual/norm: ledger `L007` (DeepNet residual scaling under poly-ALiBi) and several drafts
  probe exactly the pre-norm/residual locus this recipe fixes.
- Most `use_*` structural flags are **step-0 inert on our champion** (draft
  `flag-inertness-on-champion`) — i.e. at tiny scale, many recipe-level levers don't even
  move the model, which is itself a finding about *where* the recipe's value lives.

## Falsifier

The recipe (as a default) is bounded if a *different* bundle wins decisively and repeatably
at frontier scale across labs — i.e. the convergence breaks. For an individual component:
an in-scope, paired, noise-clearing loss win for the older alternative (e.g. RoPE loses to a
learnable additive penalty at the *same* scale it was adopted) bounds that component's claim.
