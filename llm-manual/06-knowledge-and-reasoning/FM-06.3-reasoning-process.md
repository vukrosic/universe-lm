# FM-06.3 — Models learn a genuine, probe-able reasoning process

**Rule.** On controlled grade-school math, LLMs develop a **real reasoning process**, not
template memorization: they compute intermediate quantities in correct dependency order
(level-1), and the better ones **mentally pre-plan which quantities are needed before writing
any answer** (level-2) — a skill detectable by linear probes *before* the model emits a token,
and one humans don't typically use. Reasoning errors are often decided *before* generation
begins.

**Confidence.** **[F] Frontier.** Ye, Xu, Li, Allen-Zhu (ICLR 2025, Part 2.1). Mechanistically
deep and convincing, but single-program on a synthetic math generator (iGSM); independent
replication pending.

**Scope.** Synthetic grade-school math (iGSM generator, designed so answers can't be
memorized); decoder LLMs. Claims are about *this* problem class, probed internally.

## Evidence / details

- **Not memorization.** iGSM produces effectively unlimited unique problems → high accuracy
  can't come from template recall; the model must compute.
- **Level-1 reasoning:** computes necessary quantities step-by-step in correct topological
  (dependency) order.
- **Level-2 reasoning:** *before generating*, the model has already represented **which**
  quantities are necessary — a planning skill, visible via linear probing of hidden states.
  Notably "superhuman" in form: people usually don't pre-plan the full dependency set.
- **Error mechanism:** many mistakes are determined in the hidden state *before* the first
  output token — the model "decided wrong" internally, then generated a fluent wrong chain.
- **Depth matters for reasoning:** solving deeper problems requires sufficient model *depth*
  (not just width/params) — reasoning has a structural, not purely capacity, requirement.

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

Two practical implications: (1) **depth is a reasoning resource** distinct from raw parameter
count — relevant when allocating a fixed budget toward reasoning-heavy use; (2) since errors
form pre-generation, *interventions on the hidden plan* (and training that shapes it) matter
more than post-hoc output filtering. It also gives a clean, mechanistic counter to "LLMs only
pattern-match."

## Reproduced by us?

**No — and our depth axis makes it interesting.** Our models are tiny (1–3M) and shallow, on
real text, so we don't test reasoning. But "reasoning needs depth" rhymes with our own
*structural* interest in residual routing and stacking depth (ledger work on `resid_mode`,
stacking). The honest gap: we measure next-token loss, not reasoning; bridging would require a
synthetic reasoning probe outside our current frame. Background, not an action item.

## Falsifier

Showing that high iGSM accuracy is achievable by template/lookup (no probe-able intermediate
computation), or that no pre-generation plan is detectable in models that nonetheless reason
correctly, would break the claim.
