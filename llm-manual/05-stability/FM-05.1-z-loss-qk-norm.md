# FM-05.1 — Control logit growth with z-loss + qk-norm

**Rule.** To train stably at higher learning rates (and to keep large models from diverging),
add **qk-norm** (LayerNorm on Q and K before the attention dot-product) to cap attention-logit
growth, and **z-loss** (`ζ·log²Z`, Z = softmax normalizer) to keep output logits near the
log-probabilities. Both are cheap, standard, and let you push LR higher before divergence.

**Confidence.** **[E] Established.** Mechanisms identified and mitigations validated at scale;
the instabilities and fixes both reproduce in small models.

**Scope.** Dense transformer pre-training; demonstrated from small proxies up to multi-B
models. Effective range of the fixes is LR/scale-dependent (LR sensitivity still rises with
scale even *with* the fixes).

## Evidence / details

- **qk-norm.** Without it, the divergence LR *shrinks* as models grow. With it, a **1.2B model
  trains stably at LR 0.3** and shows far lower LR sensitivity. It directly prevents attention
  logits from saturating the softmax to one-hot (which kills gradient flow).
- **z-loss.** Originally from classification; added to LLM/MoE training as an auxiliary penalty
  `L = lm_loss + ζ·log²Z` that pulls the softmax normalizer toward 0, preventing late-training
  output-logit divergence. Small `ζ` (e.g. 1e-4) is typical.
- **Reproducible small.** Wortsman et al. show both instabilities appear in *small* models at
  high LR, and the scale mitigations work there too — so you can de-risk a recipe cheaply.
- **Beyond attention:** norm growth affects QKV/Proj/FC2 outputs broadly; combining QKV-norm +
  softmax capping raised the stable LR ~1.5× over qk-norm alone (Methods of Improving LLM
  Training Stability, 2024).

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

These two additions are most of the difference between "my big run mysteriously diverged at
step 40k" and a clean run. They also *enable higher LR*, which speeds training — stability and
speed are the same lever here.

## Reproduced by us?

**Not yet — and this is the single most reproducible section for us.** Unlike scaling or data
rules, the logit-growth instabilities are *provokable at our scale* (1–3M params, high LR). qk-
norm and a z-loss term are **structural / loss-level** levers — squarely inside the ledger's
scope (RULE 0 forbids optimizer/LR mining, but qk-norm and z-loss are architecture/loss
changes, not optimizer knobs). A paired test of "does qk-norm change the champion's loss / LR-
divergence threshold at tiny scale" is a clean, in-scope ledger candidate. **Strongest
field→ledger bridge in this manual.**

## Falsifier

An in-scope high-LR run where adding qk-norm and z-loss does *not* raise the divergence LR or
stabilize training would bound the rule at that scale.
