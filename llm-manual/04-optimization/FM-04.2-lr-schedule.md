# FM-04.2 — Warmup, then decay, tuned to the actual run length

**Rule.** Use a short **linear warmup** (≈0.1–2% of steps) to the peak LR, then **decay** over
the rest of training. Two strong choices: classic **cosine decay to ~10% of peak**, or
**linear decay-to-zero (D2Z)** which beats cosine under a well-tuned peak. The decay must
reach its minimum at your **actual final step** — a schedule sized for a different run length
is meaningfully suboptimal.

**Confidence.** **[E] Established** for "warmup + decay tuned to length"; the D2Z-beats-cosine
refinement is **[C]** (one strong recent line, growing adoption).

**Scope.** Standard pre-training. The "tune to length" constraint is why **WSD (Warmup-Stable-
Decay)** is popular for continual/open-ended training where you don't know the length up front.

## Evidence / details

- **Warmup's job is stability, not final accuracy.** It lets AdamW's moment estimates settle
  and lets you use a *larger* peak LR by steering through the early high-curvature regime.
  Recent analysis: warmup's benefit is enabling aggressive peak LRs, not a direct accuracy gain.
- **Decay to length.** Using a fraction of a longer cosine, or stretching a short one, is
  measurably worse than a schedule whose minimum lands on the last step (Hoffmann/Rae lineage).
- **D2Z (linear decay to zero).** Under an optimal peak LR, simple linear→0 consistently beats
  cosine→10%: an abrupt/early LR drop under-weights late updates; smoothly→0 weights the whole
  trajectory. (Bergsma et al. 2025.)
- **WSD.** Long constant plateau + short sharp cooldown — decouples schedule from a committed
  length, good for continual pre-training (MiniCPM popularized it).
- Min/final LR: cosine→10% of peak is classic; some go peak/20 or all the way to 0 (D2Z).

## Reproduced by us?

**Held fixed, by RULE 0.** The schedule is part of the fixed optimizer background; structural
Δs are measured at a constant schedule so the lever isn't confounded by re-tuning.

## Falsifier

For "tune to length": a regime where a length-mismatched schedule matches the length-tuned one
within noise. For D2Z: an in-scope, peak-LR-tuned comparison where cosine→10% beats linear→0.
