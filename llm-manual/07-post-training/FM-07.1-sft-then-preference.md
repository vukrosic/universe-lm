# FM-07.1 — SFT first, then preference optimization (DPO or RLHF)

**Rule.** Turn a base model into an assistant in two stages: **(1) Supervised Fine-Tuning
(SFT)** on high-quality instruction→response pairs, then **(2) preference optimization** —
**DPO** (simpler, no separate reward model, now the open-model default) or **RLHF/PPO** (reward
model + RL, more complex, historically higher ceiling). Newer variants (GRPO, ORPO, KTO) sit in
this second slot.

**Confidence.** **[E] Established** for the two-stage SFT→preference pipeline; the *choice of*
preference method is **[C]** and moving fast.

**Scope.** Aligning pretrained decoder LLMs into instruction-followers/assistants. Data-hungry
in *quality*, not quantity — curation dominates.

## Evidence / details

- **SFT** (InstructGPT lineage): supervised imitation of good answers; scalable and the
  near-universal first step. Establishes basic instruction-following.
- **RLHF**: fit a reward model from human preference pairs, then optimize the policy with PPO.
  Powerful but heavy — multiple models in the loop, online sampling, finicky tuning. Hard to
  scale to niche domains (annotation cost).
- **DPO**: skips the reward model — optimize the policy *directly* on preference pairs via a
  closed-form objective. Adopted by Llama-3, Mixtral. Much simpler; the open-model default.
- **Successors:** **GRPO** (group-relative, prominent in reasoning/RL post-training), **ORPO**
  (folds preference into SFT, one stage), **KTO** (utility-style, robust to noisy/unpaired
  labels). All occupy the stage-2 slot; best choice is task- and data-dependent.
- **Pitfall — catastrophic forgetting:** naive fine-tuning can erase pretrained capability;
  mitigate with data mixing / staged or regularized objectives.
- **Data > method:** across methods, careful preference-data curation dominates the marginal
  gain from swapping algorithms.

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

This is the cheapest, highest-visibility stage — a small amount of well-curated SFT + DPO data
transforms a raw base model into something usable. For a resource-constrained team, **DPO is
the pragmatic default**: most of RLHF's benefit, a fraction of its machinery.

## Reproduced by us?

**No — out of scope.** The ledger is a pretraining structural lab; we don't post-train.
Documented for manual completeness. (Link to FM-06.2: post-training can't add facts pretraining
didn't make extractable — it shapes behavior, not knowledge.)

## Falsifier

Not a single-experiment claim. The pipeline would be bounded if direct preference optimization
*without* a prior SFT stage reliably matched SFT→preference across tasks (collapsing the two
stages) — some one-stage methods (ORPO) probe exactly this.
