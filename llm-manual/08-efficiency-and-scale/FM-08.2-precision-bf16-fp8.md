# FM-08.2 — BF16 is the safe default; FP8 works with care

**Rule.** Train in **mixed precision**: keep a master copy and the sensitive ops in higher
precision, run the bulk of matmuls in low precision. **BF16** (with FP32 accumulation) is the
robust default. **FP8** roughly doubles throughput again but needs **fine-grained (per-tile/
per-channel) scaling**, **high-precision accumulation**, and **selective retention** of
sensitive modules (embeddings, output head, norms, attention softmax, MoE gating) in BF16/FP32.

**Confidence.** **[C] Contested/Emerging.** BF16 mixed precision is [E]-level standard. FP8
end-to-end training is proven at frontier scale (DeepSeek-V3) but still requires careful,
not-yet-commoditized engineering — hence [C] for the FP8 part.

**Scope.** Transformer pre-training on hardware with BF16/FP8 support (H100/H800-class for FP8).
FP8 stability tactics are from the DeepSeek-V3 technical report; details vary by stack.

## Evidence / details

- **Mixed precision foundations.** Micikevicius et al. 2018 established the pattern: FP32 master
  weights + loss scaling + low-precision compute, matching full-precision accuracy at ~half the
  memory/bandwidth. BF16 (wide exponent, fewer mantissa bits) became the LLM default because its
  range avoids the overflow/underflow that plagued FP16 — usually **no loss scaling needed**.
- **FP8 formats.** Micikevicius et al. 2022 ("FP8 Formats for Deep Learning") standardized
  **E4M3** (more mantissa, for forward/weights) and **E5M2** (more range, for gradients),
  validated up to 175B-param transformers.
- **FP8 at scale (DeepSeek-V3).** Trained the bulk of GEMMs in FP8 with: (1) **fine-grained
  quantization** (per-tile/per-group scales) to tame outliers; (2) **promotion to higher-
  precision accumulation** to stop error buildup; (3) **keeping sensitive components**
  (embedding, output head, MoE gating, norms, attention operators) in BF16/FP32. Result: a
  competitive 671B model trained for ~2.788M H800-GPU-hours.
- **Stability link (FM-05.1):** lower precision narrows the margin before logit-growth
  instabilities bite; precision and stability tactics are co-dependent. FP32 is the most robust,
  BF16 next; FP8 trades margin for throughput.
- **Inference vs training:** post-training **int8/int4 quantization** for *inference* is a
  separate, more mature lever (FM-06.1: int8 ≈ free for knowledge, int4 lossy).

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

Precision is a direct throughput/cost multiplier. BF16 is non-negotiable table stakes (2× over
FP32, basically free). FP8 is the current frontier lever for another ~2× — worth it at large
scale if you can afford the engineering, skippable for a first model where BF16 is plenty.

## Reproduced by us?

**No — systems lever, out of scope.** Our tiny runs use the box's default precision; precision
is not a structural mechanism we mine. Relevant only via the FM-05.1 stability coupling.

## Falsifier

For the FP8 claim: a setting where FP8 (with the above tactics) fails to match BF16 final loss
at equal tokens, or where the recovery tactics don't close the gap, would bound it. For BF16:
essentially settled.
