# FM-08.3 — Extend context by interpolating RoPE, then fine-tune

**Rule.** To run a RoPE model (FM-03.1) on sequences longer than it was trained on, **don't
extrapolate** (it collapses) — **interpolate the position encoding** so long positions map back
into the trained range, then briefly fine-tune. The method progression: linear **Position
Interpolation** → **NTK-aware** (frequency-dependent) → **YaRN** (best efficiency). And
remember: a bigger window ≠ used well.

**Confidence.** **[C] Contested.** The interpolate-don't-extrapolate principle is [E]-solid and
universally used; *which* method is best and how far you can push it are evolving, hence [C].

**Scope.** RoPE-based decoder LLMs. Methods demonstrated extending LLaMA-class models from a few
K to 32K–128K tokens with modest fine-tuning.

## Evidence / details

- **Why naive extrapolation fails:** beyond the trained length, RoPE produces rotation angles
  the model never saw → attention breaks.
- **Position Interpolation (PI)** — Chen et al. 2023: linearly down-scale position indices to
  the original window (positions ÷ scale factor). Extended LLaMA to **32,768 tokens** with small
  fine-tuning. Weakness: uniform compression blurs high-frequency (local) detail.
- **NTK-aware scaling** (community origin): scale RoPE frequencies *non-uniformly* (change the
  base θ) — interpolate low frequencies more, leave high frequencies ~untouched, preserving
  local resolution. Can extend somewhat *without* fine-tuning.
- **YaRN** — Peng et al. 2023: "NTK-by-parts" (high freq extrapolated, low interpolated, mid
  blended) + an attention-**temperature** correction. **10× fewer tokens and 2.5× fewer steps**
  than PI to reach a given window. The current efficient default.
- **The usage caveat — "Lost in the Middle"** (Liu et al. 2023): models use info at the
  **start/end** of a long context far better than the **middle**. A larger window does not mean
  uniform use of it — evaluate with placement-sensitive tests, not just needle-in-haystack.

## Why it's load-bearing for a builder

Long context is mostly a *post-pretraining* extension step, not a from-scratch decision — you
train at a manageable length, then extend cheaply with YaRN. The trap is believing the
advertised window: budget evaluation for *middle-of-context* performance, and don't pay for
context the model can't actually use.

## Reproduced by us?

**No — out of scope.** Our runs are short-context tiny models; context extension is a
large-model post-hoc lever. (Tangential thread: our champion uses learnable ALiBi, not RoPE —
ALiBi has its own, different extrapolation story, so these RoPE-specific methods wouldn't apply
to it directly. See FM-03.1.)

## Falsifier

The principle is bounded if a method extends context with *quality matching native long-context
training* and *no* placement degradation (closing the Lost-in-the-Middle gap) — that would make
extension a free lunch rather than a tradeoff.
