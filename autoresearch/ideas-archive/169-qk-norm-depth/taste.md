# Taste log — 169 qk-norm-depth

## r1 — 2026-06-14 — verdict: accept
- **Leverage.** Cheap extension of 016 (the only QK-side WIN), +12 scalar params, ~15 LoC, leaves the strong 016-WIN baseline intact as the starting point. If anything, this is the natural "how much do we compound on 016?" follow-up.
- **Info value is symmetric.** A WIN (Δ over 016's already-WIN) tells us depth-conditional scale is a binding axis on top of the shared scale — and the per-block schedule the optimizer finds is a paper-able artifact. A clean NULL (Δ inside noise band, ctrl-validation clean) tells us 016's per-head-shared scale is already sufficient at 0.94M and the depth-conditional axis is not the binding one — clean attribution, not a wasted slot.
- **Distinct from 161-dyt-temp (NULL, closed).** 161 fought the canonical QK-scale prior from scratch (init `1/sqrt(d_k)`, gradient has to relearn the scale per layer). 169 starts scale-aligned at α_l=1.0 from a 016-WIN position, so the per-block lever is a small adjustment on top of an already-good scale, not a fight against the prior. Different mechanism, different cost.
- **Distinct from 152 / 155.** 152 is per-head additive logit bias, 155 is per-head multiplicative temperature on attention logits — both per-head scalars on the logits. 169 is per-block scale on the *pre-QK-matmul normalization output*, a different axis. Not a re-roll of a closed null.
- **Portfolio fit.** Active QK-norm follow-ups: 162 (which side, Q), 165 (which side, K), 169 (depth-conditional). Each tests a different axis of the 016 WIN — not crowded.
- **Crisp bet.** "Different blocks have different attention statistics (shallow broader, deep sharper) so a single shared scale may be sub-optimal; per-block learnable α_l should improve on 016's per-head-shared scale." Sharp, falsifiable, ties to NormFormer's per-layer gains.
- **Niche fit.** Mechanism (not an HP). Identity-init-able (α_l=1.0, step-0 bit-identical to 016's per-head-shared-1.0 forward). Will show its effect at tiny1m3m. Fits the parameter-golf tier.
- **Transfer.** RMSNorm family well-validated at 1B+ (LLaMA 3, Qwen 2.5, Mistral). NormFormer's per-layer gains are the closest direct analog and are validated at 100M+ on long-document tasks. Low transfer risk — even if 169 NULLs at 0.94M, the primitive is worth carrying toward 135M.
- **Minor note for the definition gate:** spec should lock step-0 identity to 016's forward (not to the no-norm baseline), with the same fp32 max-abs-diff < 1e-3 tolerance as 016. The control must be 016's WIN config, not unmodded-nanogpt, so the Δ is depth-conditional-on-016 not "QK-norm yes/no".
