# Taste log — 199 spectral-attn-output

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: revise
- **Crowded family, walked into a closed axis.** The post-AV / per-head-attention-shape null family is already 4 deep at tiny1m3m: 160-rms-gain-per-head (Δ=-0.0023 inside band), 142-layerscale (Δ=-0.0012, 5th depth-conditional null), 181-cross-head-rmsnorm (Δ=+0.1722 wrong-sign), 176-v-pre-av-norm (Δ=+0.0303 inside band). The 128-spectral-decoupling null is the gradient-space twin of 199's W_O Lipschitz framing (Δ=+0.10 wrong-sign, ~20× noise band). The "spectral cap on attn output" branch of 199 is mechanically a sibling of 160 (per-head scalar init 1) — the miner even concedes "essentially 160-rms-gain-per-head closed null". To be worth a slot, 199 must be **structurally distinct** from 160 in a load-bearing way, not "Lipschitz interpretation" of the same lever. The miner needs to spell out what axis 199 opens that 160 didn't — e.g. constraint on the projection (W_O) rather than gain on its output is a real difference, but the current pitch doesn't cash that out into a prediction.
- **Asymmetric `min(1, cap/σ_max)` is structurally a damage-only lever.** The proposed 0-param formulation `W_O_eff = W_O · min(1, wo_spectral_cap / σ_max(W_O))` can only ever scale W_O down (or be a no-op). It has no learnable axis, so the optimizer cannot "discover" the right tightness. Power-iteration overhead runs every step to compute σ_max for a cap that almost certainly won't bind at 0.94M/12L (per 160: "gradient signal is too weak at 0.94M/3M tokens to push the gains away from 1"). Re-formulate with a learnable cap: e.g. `wo_spectral_cap = σ_max(W_O_init) · exp(γ_h_global)` with γ_h_global init 0 ⇒ byte-identical baseline, optimizer can loosen or tighten. Asymmetric is fine if the asymmetry is the bet, but the bet must be stated.
- **No sharp prediction.** The pitch says "a 199 WIN would mean Lipschitz control on W_O binds at 0.94M" but offers no magnitude. The closed sibling nulls all sit inside |Δ|<0.005; a realistic bet is also inside that band, which means the A/B is structurally low-leverage. Either: (a) name a concrete Δval window with mechanism (e.g. "σ_max(W_O) grows ~1.5× over the 92-step run, clipping to init should preserve attn-output gradient signal → Δval in [-0.005, -0.010]"), or (b) accept that the bet is sub-noise and add the information-value argument: a clean null at 0.94M would close the post-attention-shape null family at 5 deep. Either is acceptable; the current "vibes + walkthrough" is neither.
- **Branch to drop.** The early "per-token spectral norm on attention output" branch (out / (||out||_2 + ε)) is explicitly not byte-identical at step 0, which the miner already flags. Drop it; keep only the W_O spectral-norm-regularization branch, properly re-formulated with a learnable axis.
