- **Mechanism is a mechanism, not HP-tuning.** Per-block learnable scalar `γ_l` (init 0) inside an `exp(·)` wrapper on the W_O spectral cap is a structural regularization axis on a specific projection — step-0 identity (byte-identical by construction since `exp(0)=1` and the cached `σ_max(W_O_init)` is the current `σ_max` at init). Forward effect: `W_O_eff = W_O · min(1, σ_max_init·exp(γ_l) / σ_max_current)`. This is weight-space Lipschitz regularization, not a learning-rate or schedule lever. ✓

**One implementation note for the code-implementer (not a finding).** When caching `σ_max(W_O_init)` at init time, snapshot it once on the first forward (before any optimizer step) and freeze as a buffer — recomputing per-step would defeat the "byte-identical at step 0" guarantee and silently shift σ_max_init across runs. Power-iteration state `u` should be a non-parameter buffer (`.buffers()` not `.parameters()`) so it survives optimizer state serialization but does not consume optimizer slots.
