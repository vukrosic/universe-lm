## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: approve

- **Source is real and current.** RoFormer / RoPE (Su et al. 2024, arXiv:2104.09864) and Shi et al. 2024 (arXiv:2407.06641, 154's source) are both verified; RoPE's per-pair 2D rotation is the parameterization primitive 200 inherits. No fabricated citation. In-repo priors cited (154 WIN, 172 null, 175 WIN, depth-axis cluster 161/142/130/111/116, 185 procedural-closed) all real and accurately characterized.
- **Mechanism is a mechanism, not a hyperparameter.** The r1 default sketch (1 φ per layer, applied to both Q and K) was a provable no-op (R orthogonal on both sides preserves `<Rq, Rk> = <q, k>` exactly), and the r2 fix is mechanically correct: applying the per-layer × per-pair 2D rotation **to K only** breaks the QK^T inner-product preservation (Q is in basis R(m·θ_i), K is in basis R_l · R(m·θ_i); the score = Q(0)^T · (R(−m·θ_i) · R_l · R(m·θ_i)) · K(0) is a position-dependent conjugation of R_l, which equals I only when R_l = I). Init φ_{l,i} = 0 ⇒ R_l = I in fp32 (bit-exact `cos(0)=1, sin(0)=0`) ⇒ K unchanged ⇒ QK^T unchanged ⇒ loss byte-identical at step 0. The orthogonal-block-diagonal structure preserves K's norm and the softmax temperature. Architectural change, not an LR/schedule/init-constant lever.
- **tiny1m3m only.** All references and pass-bar math are tiny1m3m (0.94M · 3M tok), seed 42, single cache reference `5b8a7fea8963` (val 6.3988, band 0.04, 3 measurements). No screen20m or larger-tier leakage. ✅
- **Not already closed.** 200 is genuinely fresh:
  - **154 (WIN)** — fixed *random shared* rebase of K and V. 200 is *learned, per-layer × per-pair, K-only*. Tests whether the optimizer can find a *per-layer × per-pair* axis that the *random* 154 axis couldn't. Different lever form.
  - **172 (null)** — per-head *frequency* on RoPE's position-dependent angle `θ_i(m)` (the slope of angle-vs-position changes per head, but stays position-dependent). 200 is per-layer *phase* (constant offset, position-independent) on K post-RoPE. Different tensor (K post-RoPE, not Q/K angles pre-rotation), different operation (rotation, not angle scaling), different axis (per-layer, not per-head). 172's null is *not* prior on 200.
  - **175 (WIN, Δ=−0.1585)** — per-head additive bias on attention scores. 200 is per-layer × per-pair rotation of K. 175 is the precedent for "pre-softmax, post-Q/K-projection, W_O-non-absorbable" levers binding at 0.94M; 200 is in the same structural category with a different op (rotation vs bias).
  - **185 (procedurally closed)** — per-head × per-pair, 384 params. The closed.md entry is "reject: exhausted 3 recode rounds (MAX_RECODE_ROUNDS=3), axis abandoned" — that's an *implementation-attempt* closure, not an *empirical* closure. Per `185/evidence.md` + `185/log.jsonl`, the box's `git pull` couldn't see the locally-added `Tiny1M3MStaticKRotationConfig` class across all 4 attempts; SMOKE_FAIL on every attempt; no GPU time, no val measured. The lever family is empirically open. 200 lives in a different cell of the (depth × head) grid: 185 = head × pair (H=4, P=8, 32 angles/block, varies across heads within a layer), 200 = layer × pair (L=12, P=8, 96 angles total, varies across layers, shared across heads). 200 is *smaller* (96 vs 384 params) and *more parsimonious* — a strict subset of 185's parameterization axes.
  - **Depth-axis null cluster (161/142/130/111/116)** — all scalar per-layer params, all absorbable by W_O (a per-layer scalar α_l on the residual path is degenerate with W_O's per-channel structure at d_model=64). 200 is *rotational* per-layer × per-pair, and the per-pair structure breaks the cross-pair symmetry that W_O's per-channel projection has no equivalent operation for. The "W_O absorbability" defense is sound and matches the project's 175-alibi precedent for pre-softmax W_O-non-absorbable levers binding.
