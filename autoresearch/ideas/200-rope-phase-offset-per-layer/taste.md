# Taste log — 200 rope-phase-offset-per-layer

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: revise

The mechanism has a clean shape on paper (init 0 = byte-identical, +12 to +48 params, identity-init-able) but the math plus the recent closed list together kill the bet as written. Two critical findings, one of them fatal to the default sketch.

- **CRITICAL — default sketch (1 φ per layer) is a mathematical no-op.** The mechanism writes `θ_i(m, l) = m · base^(-2i/d_k) + φ_l`, applied to **both** Q and K. Since 2D rotation matrices compose, `R(θ_standard + φ_l) = R(φ_l) · R(θ_standard)`, so the lever is: apply standard RoPE, then apply a fixed per-layer 2D-rotation block-diagonal `R(φ_l)` (all 8 pairs share the same angle) to both Q and K. The same fixed orthogonal `R(φ_l)` on both Q and K preserves QK^T exactly (`<Rq, Rk> = <q, k>` for orthogonal R), so attention scores are unchanged, softmax is unchanged, AV is unchanged. The downstream W_O can absorb `R(φ_l)` into a new equivalent W_O' (orthogonal R does not change W_O's effective spectral norm). Net: the 12-scalar version of 200 changes the forward graph but not the loss, and the optimizer has zero gradient on φ_l. The design sketch's first option is a no-op A/B, which would burn a slot for a guaranteed null. The miner must either (a) explain why the no-op claim fails (some non-obvious cross-pair or W_O interaction I'm missing), or (b) commit to the per-pair sketch (φ_l_i for each of the 4 pairs in d_k=16), where per-pair φ breaks the orthogonal symmetry across pairs and CAN have an effect.

- **CRITICAL — per-pair sketch is structurally a less-expressive 185.** 185-static-per-head-k-rotation is exactly "static orthogonal rotation on K, parameterized by learnable angles, identity-init". 185 had 4 heads × 8 planes = 32 angles per block × 12 blocks = 384 params (H × d_k/2). With per-pair φ (4 planes × 12 blocks = 48 angles), 200 is the same lever family, just with: (i) per-pair only, not per-head (4× less expressive on the per-head axis), (ii) applied to both Q and K, not K only (more constrained, since QK^T sees the same rotation on both sides — net rotation that affects AV must propagate through QK^T asymmetry between pairs, which is a thinner axis than 185's per-head K-only). 185 exhausted 3 recode rounds and the axis was abandoned. The miner pitches 200 as "different from 185 (RoPE-modulating vs post-RoPE)", but the math is the same lever family. If 185 with 32 scalars per block couldn't bind across 3 recode attempts, 200 with 4 scalars per block on a more constrained (QK-symmetric) axis is the wrong direction. Either the miner engages with 185's outcome directly — was the failure a recode plumbing issue (ImportError on the box per the `idea.md` re-code note) or a real null — and argues why the QK-symmetric per-pair version is qualitatively different, or the lever is dead.

- **Prior evidence: 172 closes the position-axis at 0.94M for the per-head-frequency variant.** 172-per-head-rope-base (per-head frequency, 48 scalars) closed null at tiny1m3m (Δ=+0.0109 wrong-sign tiny). 200 is on the same position-encoding axis (it modifies the RoPE angle), at strictly less expressiveness (per-layer 12 scalars vs per-head 48 scalars in 172). The 172 prior says: scalar modulations of RoPE angles don't bind at this tier. The miner dismisses 172 as "different axis (frequency vs phase)", but at 0.94M with 92 update steps, "different axis" reads as a distinction that the optimizer can't see — the binding constraint is total scalar count, not which RoPE parameter they touch.

- **Prior evidence: depth-axis null cluster is damning.** Recent per-layer scalars at 0.94M have all closed null: 161-dyt-temp (per-layer τ — the `idea.md` literally said "at 0.94M/12L the model has no scale-depth for the lever to find a useful different-per-layer schedule"), 142-layerscale, 130-rezero, 111-drop-path, 116-hyper-connections. The pattern: 12-layer depth at 0.94M has no useful per-layer specialization axis. 200's per-layer φ_l falls into this cluster. The 175-alibi WIN is the *counter-example* that the miner will cite, but 175 is a *uniform* per-head bias, not a *per-layer* variation — different axis, different finding.

- **Niche fit otherwise OK in principle**: mechanism (yes), identity-init (yes for the no-op case trivially, yes for the per-pair case at θ=0), tiny1m3m runnable, +0.001% params, RoPE angle structure is a real lever family at 7B+ (LLaMA 1/2/3, Mistral, Qwen, etc.), so the transfer argument would be strong *if* the lever could bind at 0.94M. The transfer story doesn't rescue a no-op or a near-superset of an abandoned lever — both fail before transfer matters.

- **Crisp bet is missing the strongest 2 priors.** The "Why it's worth a slot" paragraph cites 172-null and 175-WIN but doesn't engage with 185's recode-cap (closed 2026-06-15) or the 1-scalar-per-layer no-op. Without engaging these, the bet is built on priors the miner hasn't read.

- **What the reviser must do to make this sharp.** (1) Disambiguate: commit to the per-pair sketch (48 scalars, breaks the orthogonal symmetry), and drop the 12-scalar default as broken. (2) Read 185's `taste.md`, `idea.md`, and `review.md` (it was accepted at the taste gate, so the bet that "rebase axis is real" passed taste — engage with where 185 actually died). (3) Argue in one sentence why per-pair QK-symmetric rotation would bind where 185's per-head K-only rotation did not, given 185 is strictly more expressive on the per-head axis and less constrained on the QK-symmetry axis. (4) Acknowledge the depth-axis null cluster and explain why a *rotational* per-layer parameter is qualitatively different from a *scalar* per-layer parameter — the rotational lever breaks QK^T orthogonality across pairs, which scalar per-layer levers (161/142/130) do not. A WIN here is informative; a NULL is also informative (it bounds the RoPE-modulation axis). The bet only earns a slot if the math is right and the prior-engagement is real.

- **Verdict: revise** — the default sketch is mathematically a no-op, the per-pair sketch overlaps 185 (just abandoned), and the bet doesn't engage the strongest priors. With the math fix + 185 engagement + depth-axis argument, this could re-pitch as a sharp per-pair lever; without those, the next round is a `reject`.
