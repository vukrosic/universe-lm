- **The pre-softmax form is exactly 155 (closed null) — drop it.** The author concedes it themselves: "Actually these ARE algebraically equivalent with reparameterization. So 205 is a *re-parameterization* of 155 — different parameterization but same mathematical function." The closed `155-per-head-temp` line (closed.md) reads: "three different per-head-attention-shape levers (norm on Q/K, bias on QK^T logits, scalar on logits temperature) all close null at this tier" and the cross-null on 152-attn-logit-bias and 160-rms-gain-per-head gives a *three-lever* closed pattern. - **The post-softmax form *is* mathematically distinct — but the bet is a vibe.** `attn_h_post = τ_h · attn_h + (1 − τ_h) · (1/T)` with τ_h init 1 ⇒ bit-identical baseline is real (different optimization landscape from pre-softmax: bounded, interpolates with uniform, has a hard lower bound at 1/T, no "break the softmax" failure mode). But the bet sentence — "at 0.94M, the post-softmax axis (where attention weights are re-distributed) binds differently from the pre-softmax axis (where logits are scaled)" — is a placement-assertion, not a mechanism-level prediction. *Why* would boundedness matter at this scale? Without that, the slot is "try post-softmax, see what happens" — exactly the vibe the protocol marks for `revise`. Sharpen: pick one mechanism-level claim — e.g. (a) "the boundedness fixes the *failure mode* that drags 155 into null (τ_h grows unboundedly and breaks per-head softmax normalization), so 205 is 155 minus the failure mode" or (b) "post-softmax interpolation is a *post-norm* lever — the QK axis binds at 0.94M (016 WIN), the post-AV axis doesn't (160 NULL); the post-softmax axis is in between, a tighter test of which softmax-side axis binds at this tier" or (c) "heads that want to soften attention can only soften to uniform 1/T in 205, not below — this asymmetric boundedness is the lever's only advantage over 155, and we expect τ_h ≤ 1 to be the dominant signal at convergence". Pick one and quantify.

- **Portfolio is crowded; per-head-attention-shape is the most-tested family in the queue.** Active cluster: 152 (per-head bias, null), 155 (per-head temp, null), 160 (per-head post-AV gain, null), 166 (T5 RPE, null), 167 (logit z-loss, null), 177 (talking-heads, drift), 180 (qk logit conv, leak). 184-logit-scale was accepted *specifically* because it broke the symmetry — the OUTPUT-side scalar cannot be absorbed by per-head Q/K updates the way 152/155/160 are. 205 stays in the *attention-side* family and offers no symmetry-breaking argument; it is the 4th+ per-head-attention-shape variant the queue has seen in the last 10 days. The case for the slot has to be: a) a clean symmetry-breaking argument vs 152/155/160 (which the boundedness-of-205 could be, if argued properly), and b) a sharp prediction that distinguishes a 205 win from a 205 null in a way 155's null didn't already show. Frame the slot as: "if win → bounded per-head attention-shape is the missing axis; if null → per-head attention-shape is dead at 0.94M regardless of placement (extends 152/155/160 to placement-axis)".
