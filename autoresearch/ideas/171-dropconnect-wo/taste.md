# Taste log — 171 dropconnect-wo

## r1 — 2026-06-14 — verdict: revise
- **Treatment ambiguity is a taste issue, not a definition issue.** The design sketch lists three options (A: rate=0.1 immediately → breaks byte-identity; B: rate=0.0 throughout → vacuous A/B, regularizer never fires; C: ramp 0.0 → 0.05 over 100 steps → byte-identical at step 0, lever fires later) and leaves all three on the table. The "we expect X because Y" sentence is therefore undefined until one is locked. Lock **(C): schedule rate from 0.0 to 0.05 over the first 100 steps** as the canonical treatment. This is the only option that simultaneously satisfies (i) step-0 byte-identity and (ii) the regularizer actually doing something. (B) tests "infrastructure is in place" — that's a definition-gate concern, not a lever test, and would burn a slot for an info-poor A/B. State this explicitly in the re-pitch.
- **Engage the prior 3 regularizer nulls at 0.94M, don't just mention them.** Closest priors are 147-dropkey (per-token K-mask, null), 111-drop-path (per-branch mask, null), and 115-rdrop (KL-divergence regularizer, null) — all three live in the closed.md regularizer block. The current pitch mentions them as different tensors but doesn't argue the *mechanism* for why weight-level noise should bind at 0.94M when token-level (147) and path-level (111) didn't. Two defensible framings: (a) weight-level is a distinct stochastic axis that the optimizer at 0.94M has no other lever to absorb (Q/K gradient updates can soak up per-token masks, but W_O updates can't mask themselves), or (b) 147/111 failed because the *targets* (K matrix, residual branches) are not co-adaptation hot spots at d_model=64/12L, but W_O is a 64×64 dense projection where co-adaptation is structurally cheap. Pick one and put a 1-paragraph mechanistic argument in the pitch.
- **Sharpen the win/null bar against the 0.04 noise band.** Expected Δval is −0.005 to −0.015, and the cached 6.4394±0.04 / 6.4504±0.0558 baseline bands put the upper bound of that range *inside* noise. Specify explicitly: a Δ ≤ −0.020 is the only signal that clears |Δ|>0.04 noise at one seed, Δ ∈ [−0.020, −0.005] is informative but inconclusive (treat as null-and-close), Δ ≥ −0.005 is null. Frame the re-pitch so the null outcome still has payoff: "null closes the weight-level axis of the regularizer family (after token-level 147 and path-level 111, weight-level 171 would complete the family exhaustion at 0.94M)."
- **Leverage is borderline but the cost is low.** d_model=64 → W_O is 64×64 = 4096 weights, a small surface for weight-level noise to fire. ~25 LoC + ~3–4 min compute + a few hours of agent attention is acceptable spend for closing an axis (per taste bar: "A clean null result must still be worth logging"). Don't dwell on the leverage concern — one sentence acknowledging d_model=64 limits the effect is enough; the family-exhaustion argument carries the rest.
- **Portfolio note (not a blocker).** This is the 4th regularizer variant after 147/111/115. Crowded but defensible: the pitch's "exhaust the family" framing is the right shape. Acceptable as-is; no need to rebrand as a non-regularizer lever.
## r2 — 2026-06-14 — verdict: accept
- **All three r1 findings genuinely addressed, no hand-wave.** Treatment locked to ramp 0.0→0.05 over the first 100 steps (with explicit byte-identity walkthrough at step 0: effective_rate=0.0 ⇒ guard false ⇒ no RNG consumed ⇒ max-abs-diff=0.0). Mechanistic argument is the strongest part of the re-pitch: "the mask is the gradient mask, not just the forward mask — the optimizer cannot route around the noise by adjusting a different parameter, because the noise is on the parameter being updated." Win/null bar sharpened against the 0.04 noise band with explicit Δ thresholds and a non-vacuous null payoff ("null closes the weight-level axis of the regularizer family"). No bar gaps left.
- **Sharp non-obvious bet.** The "parameter-space vs signal-space regularizer" framing is a real distinction, not a synonym shuffle. 147-dropkey multiplies K by a random mask (signal-space — Q gradients can absorb), 111-drop-path zeros branches (signal-space — surviving branches get clean gradients), but 171 on W_O zeros the parameter whose gradient is being computed (parameter-space — no other parameter's update can mask the noise). This is the kind of one-sentence mechanistic claim taste wants.
- **Family-exhaustion framing is the right shape.** closed.md confirms 147-dropkey (null Δ=+0.0236), 111-drop-path (DRIFT Δ=+0.05), 115-rdrop (null Δ=-0.0107), 116-hyper-connections (null Δ=+0.0666), 131-layer-drop (null), 142-layerscale (null) — six prior regularizer-side levers exhausted at 0.94M. Weight-level on a single dense projection is the only structurally-distinct stochastic axis not yet tested at this tier. Closing it is a real contribution regardless of sign.
- **Leverage is borderline but acceptable.** d_model=64 ⇒ W_O is 64×64=4096 weights, small surface — but the re-pitch's "W_O can co-adapt onto a narrow subspace over 192 steps; weight masking forces redundant paths in that single matrix" is a credible mechanistic anchor. Combined with low cost (~30 LoC, ~3-4 min compute, no scale-up retry possible per ONE-TIER-ONLY), the spend is defensible.
- **Transfer risk med is honestly framed.** Cross-tier bet is "dense projection + data-limited setup" → "W_O dense projection + 0.94M sees ~3M tokens which is data-limited for LMs." Not bulletproof (vision-CNN ≠ transformer LM), but mechanistic enough to carry the med tag rather than force a reject.
