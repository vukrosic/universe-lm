## r1 — 2026-06-09 — verdict: accept
- **Mechanism**: categorical. `DyT(x) = γ ⊙ tanh(α·x) + β` drops the per-token mean/variance reduction entirely and replaces LN with a parameter-only tanh squash. α is a single learnable scalar per DyT module; γ, β are length-d_model learnable vectors (paper Table 2 init: α₀=0.5, γ₀=1, β₀=0). Replaces every `nn.LayerNorm` / `RMSNorm` call site (pre-norms × 2 per block, final pre-out-proj norm, optional embedding norm) — ~6 sites, < 50 LoC.
- **Transferability**: categorically distinct from the closed norm-zoo (pnorm, manhattan, center, squash, clip, channelscale). The closed zoo all *kept* LN's reduce-then-normalize template and varied which statistic was subtracted — they all showed NULL. DyT is a *categorical* change: no reduction at all, no mean, no variance — just a learnable squash. The closed nulls closed "which statistic"; DyT tests "is the statistic step even needed?" Strong information value.
- **Bet** (crisp): "we expect DyT to match LN at tiny1m3m because LN's stabilization value comes from *bounding activations*, and `tanh(α·x)` does that parameter-only — the per-token mean/variance reduction is unnecessary." If true, plus a small free speedup (no all-reduce across features at our small `d_model`, ~5-8% step-time).
- **Null teaching**: a clean null at tiny1m3m confirms the closed norm-zoo result at a *categorical* level — "the *reduce* matters, not the *shape* of the post-affine; tanh alone isn't enough." This is informative because the closed nulls couldn't partition this — they only varied which statistic to reduce. DyT is the only lever that drops the reduction entirely.
- **Init concern flagged**: DyT is *not* bit-identical at step 0 (α₀=0.5 → tanh(0.5·x) ≈ 0.5x − x³/24, gain ~1 but not 1.0). The prompt's niche-fit rule is "identity/zero-init-able." DyT fails strict bit-identity, but the bet is on the model recovering within a few hundred steps — plausible because tanh is bounded and the gain is close to 1. **Not blocking**, but the reviewer should require the A/B to (a) include a "warmup" period of ~200 steps before sampling loss, or (b) compare early-step-1 and early-step-100 losses to verify the model recovers. The miner was upfront about this trade-off.
- **Portfolio**: 016 (QK-Norm) and 017 (sub-LN) are *additive* norm levers; 019 is *replacement*. Different family. Plus a categorical change tests a *new* question (do we need the reduce at all?). After 6+ norm-zoo NULLs, this is the first idea in the batch that *categorically* changes the function — accept on that basis.
- **Caveat for the reviewer**: PASS bar should be permissive (DyT loss within ~0.02 of LN), since the bet is "matches" not "beats." A null is the more informative outcome for the closed-zoo partition. Wire as a single `use_dyt` flag, sed-replace the 6 LN sites.
