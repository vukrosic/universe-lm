# Taste log — 188 qk-rms-scaling

## r2 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: accept
- **All three r1 findings are addressed in the r2 re-pitch.** (1) 161-dyt-temp is now in the defensive section as the closest closed analog with explicit mechanism comparison (plain baseline + τ_l init 1/sqrt(d_k) → DRIFT; 016 norm + s_l init 1.0 → new bet). (2) The "5th variant in a row" framing is corrected: 188 is the **first** lever that *combines* a normalization (closed WIN 016) with a temperature — 016/152/155/161/169/184 are all single-axis, none stacks a closed WIN normalization with a learnable temperature. (3) The bet is no longer about init=1 vs init=1/sqrt(d_k); it's about the *post-norm signal-to-noise ratio* of `s_l`. Init=1 is the IEEE-754 exact rest point of `exp(0)`, not the lever's pitch.
- **The new bet is genuinely distinct from 161 (not just a re-test with different init).** 161 DRIFTed for two reasons: (a) gradient fights the canonical attention-scale prior in the plain baseline, (b) the 0.94M/12L model has no scale-depth for a per-block lever to find a useful different-per-layer schedule. Stacking on 016 explicitly removes (a) — the canonical 1/sqrt(d_k) prior is absorbed by the per-head RMSNorm + per-head gain, so `s_l` no longer fights a magnitude-noise prior. It does NOT remove (b) — the model is still 0.94M/12L. The r2 bet is precisely: "reason (a) was the binding constraint in 161; removing it gives the per-block axis room to work." This is a clean, falsifiable hypothesis. If NULL/DRIFT at 0.94M, the conclusion is "reason (b) is binding" (per-block temp doesn't work at this tier regardless of normalization). If WIN, reason (a) was the obstacle.
- **Ctrl is well-defined and the experiment is clean.** Baseline = `Tiny1M3MQKNormConfig` (016 WIN, val 6.3906). Trt = 016 + per-block `s_l = exp(s_param_l)`, init 1.0, byte-identical at step 0. 12 params total (+0.0013% overhead). The bit-identity claim holds (`nn.Parameter(torch.zeros(1))` → `exp(0)=1.0` exactly in IEEE 754 → `scores * 1.0 = scores` exactly). SDPA flash path is correctly excluded for step-0 numerics.
- **Null is informative.** A DRIFT (`trt_val > ctrl_val + 0.01`) closes the *post-norm* per-block-temp axis as distinct from 161's *plain* per-block-temp axis — and would tell us reason (b) of 161's close is the binding constraint, not reason (a). A NULL (`|Δ| < 0.01`) is similarly informative. A WIN (`Δ ≤ -0.005`) would amplify the closed 016 WIN.
- **Niche fit holds.** Mechanism (learnable per-block scalar on post-QK-norm scores), not HP. Zero/identity-init-able. Fits tiny1m3m. transfer-risk: low is correct and the `## Scale evidence` section cites μTransfer (Yang 2022) per-layer logit-temperature at 10M-13B + DeepNet α as the analogous fixed rule — both are depth-conditional scale levers. The "stacking on a normalization layer is a published μP recipe (init=1, exp-parameterized)" claim is accurate.
- **Portfolio fit is acceptable.** Other needs-taste ideas are: 192-topk-attn (structural sparsity, different family), 193-mup-init (init family, different axis), 197-deepnet-α (init-time only, no learnable params). 188 is the only one combining a *closed WIN* (016) with a learnable per-block scalar. Not crowded.
- **Crisp bet sentence in the idea:** "the closed per-block temperature on the plain baseline (161) DRIFTed because the lever fought the un-normalized Q/K magnitude prior; stacked on the WIN 016-qk-norm the lever operates on a *post-norm* signal where |QK^T| is bounded and centered, so a per-block scalar has a clean axis to re-tune depth-conditional sharpness without the magnitude-noise swamping that 161 hit." That's the bar.
- **Minor concern (not blocking):** the r1 "5th variant" critique is technically *not* fully closed — 197-output-residual-sqrt-2l is also a per-block attention-shape lever on the same queue, and if 197 lands first there's some risk of correlated context. But 197 is *init-time only* (no learnable params) while 188 is *learned* (12 trainable scalars) — the axes are different and the experiment is independent. Accepting.
- **Verdict: accept** → needs-review, round reset to 1 for the definition gate's own budget. The definition gate should verify: (a) the `use_qk_rms_scaling: bool` plumbing is off by default, (b) the manual-path forcing list is updated, (c) the `Tiny1M3MQKRMSConfig` subclass overrides match the 016 parent's per-head norm behavior, (d) ctrl `Tiny1M3MQKNormConfig` is the **same** config used to validate the 016 WIN (val 6.3906) so the comparison is fair, (e) the pass/fail bars are achievable at this scale (Δ ≤ -0.005 is realistic given 016 itself was Δ=-0.0185 vs prior baseline).

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: revise
- **Critical omission: 161-dyt-temp is the same axis.** The "Distinct from closed axes" defensive section lists 155 (per-head), 152 (additive bias), 184 (LM-head output), 169 (norm placement), 016 (symmetric QK norm) — but conspicuously omits **161-dyt-temp**, which is the closest closed analog: per-layer learnable `τ_l` scalar on attention scores, log-parameterized, the *per-block* axis (one `[n_layers]` parameter), closed at tiny1m3m on 2026-06-14 with `trt=6.5150 vs fresh ctrl mean=6.4320` (Δ=+0.0830, ~2× noise band **DRIFT** on the wrong side). 188's `s_l = exp(s_param_l)` with init 0 is structurally the same lever — only the **init differs** (`1/sqrt(d_k)` in 161 vs `1.0` in 188). The "block-conditional vs head-conditional" distinction 188 leans on is exactly what 161 already tested. The null is highly predictable; the A/B is likely info-free as pitched.
- **Family is exhausted at 0.94M.** This is the **5th** attention-shape/scale lever: 016-qk-norm (WIN, sym QK norm), 152-attn-logit-bias (null, per-head additive), 155-per-head-temp (null, per-head), 161-dyt-temp (null DRIFT, per-block — the closed analog), 169-qk-norm-depth (null, placement), 184-logit-scale (output-side scalar). Per the "5th variant in a row" rule in the taste prompt, this is a `revise (diversify)` even if individually fine.
- **Init=1 vs init=1/sqrt(d_k) is an HP tweak, not a mechanism change.** Both parameterize a per-block positive scalar on pre-softmax scores. Both claim the same benefit (depth-conditional attention temperature). The only difference is where on the positive reals the initial point sits; the gradient dynamics at 92 update steps are dominated by the LR schedule and the small effective batch, not by the init in the multiplicative-positive parameterization. 188 needs a *mechanistic* argument (not an init argument) for why this would behave differently from 161 — and the 161 close note already says "gradient fights the canonical attention-scale prior; at 0.94M/12L the model has no scale-depth for the lever to find a useful different-per-layer schedule" — that conclusion holds for init=1 too (s_l starts at 1 but the gradient signal is the same).
- **Scale evidence is real but doesn't rescue 0.94M binding.** μ-Transfer (Yang 2022) per-layer logit-temperature is validated at 10M-13B; Pythia per-layer LR multiplier (Biderman 2023) is a *learning-rate* lever not a temperature lever — different mechanism. DeepNet `α = 1/sqrt(2L)` is a *fixed* depth-conditional scale, not learned. None of these say "per-block learned attention temperature binds at 0.94M"; the only direct test (161) said DRIFT.
- **What would justify an accept on a re-pitch:**
  - (a) Sharp mechanistic story for why the *exp(0)=1 init* removes a gradient prior that 161's `1/sqrt(d_k)` init failed to overcome — the lever's "bet sentence" must name the gradient-path effect, not just the parameterization.
  - (b) Pivot to a *different shape*: e.g., per-block scalar as a **multiplicative gate on the softmax temperature** *with a non-trivial init schedule* (e.g., 1.0 → 1/sqrt(d_k) over warmup) so the lever is forced through the canonical prior; or a **per-block logit-bias added** in addition to the temperature; or **stacking on 016** (the idea notes this is possible) — at which point the bet is "016+per-block temp beats 016 alone" and the null is the existing 016 baseline.
  - (c) Diversify away from the temperature family entirely and pick a different attention-shape axis (e.g., a per-block *additive* score term tied to layer index, a per-block RoPE base, a per-block QK-norm gain as in 160 but on a different axis).
- **Bit-identity holds and the design sketch is clean** — this is not a quality concern. The 12-param cost is correct. The exp(0)=1.0 IEEE-754 identity is correct. This is a taste call, not a correctness call.
- **Niche fit otherwise:** mechanism-shaped, identity-able, fits tiny1m3m, fits the 135M recipe *if* it binds. The bar isn't "the lever is well-engineered" — the bar is "the bet is non-crowded and the null is informative."
- **Verdict: revise** → needs-repitch. The miner must address 161 head-on (either with a mechanistic reframe or a different shape) before this earns a slot. If r2/r3 cannot sharpen the bet, the 3-round cap will force a reject per protocol.
