# Review log — 206 cross-block-ffn-share

## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: approve

- **Source is real and current — passing.** Universal Transformers (Dehghani et al., ICLR 2019, arXiv:1807.03819) and ALBERT (Lan et al., 2020, arXiv:1909.11942) both verified, both real, both well-validated. ALBERT at BERT-base/large/xxlarge (110M-235M) is the *closest published partial-tying precedent*. The in-repo source links (197, 188, 021) are all real and verifiable on disk.
- **Mechanism is structural, not HP — passing.** Per-block learnable scalars `α_up_b, α_down_b ∈ R` (init −10, sigmoid ⇒ ≈4.5e-5) blend `W_up_b ← (1−α)W_up_b + α W_up_{b-1}.detach()` and analogously for `W_down`. At step 0, `W_up_eff = W_up_b` exactly (bit-identical to baseline) and likewise for `W_down`. The forward graph is mutated only when α > 0, and the `.detach()` correctly prevents gradient flowing back into the previous block's W matrices. This is a structural change to the parameter-sharing graph, not an LR / schedule / init-constant knob. W_gate is correctly left per-block — the gating decision is a per-block axis, only the expansion/compression subspace is shared. Step-0 byte-identity is exact (the residual path is unchanged because `W_up_eff = W_up_b` at step 0).
- **Tiny1m3m only, single seed — passing.** The whole idea bounds to tiny1m3m (0.94M · 3M tok, seed 42). No reference to screen20m, full ladder, or multi-seed. Per ONE-SEED-ONLY, a sub-noise effect (|Δval| < 0.01) is **inconclusive, not real** — the plan must encode this.
- **Not already closed — passing.** The closed `closed.md:23` "layer tying" line is the *full* Universal-Transformer tying axis (every block shares every matrix). 206 is **narrower**: only `W_up` and `W_down` (the two largest FFN matrices, 49k each); `W_gate` and the Q/K/V/O projections stay per-block. Different lever, different lever-knob. The 197 / 188 / 021 family referenced in `idea.md` are also different matrices (W_O in attention, K/V projections, V-residual stream respectively) — the taste log's "different matrix" discrimination is correct, not a dedup smell. The closed "tied QK" line at `closed.md:23` is *QK* (post-`q/k_gain` exploration), not the FFN up/down projections. No mathematical duplicate of 206 anywhere in `closed.md`.
- **LoC budget — passing.** Pattern is identical to the already-implemented 188 cross-block-kv-share wiring (`models/layers.py:1027,1703-1708,2920-2931,4977-4985,5130,5221` for the MHA side; `models/llm.py:433-434,834,1166,1659,1817` for the threading side; `configs/llm_config.py:545,6664-6731` for the config subclass). The 206 wiring is the FFN analog: a per-block `prev_W_up`/`prev_W_down` hook stored on each block (set after init in `models/llm.py`, mirroring the 188 pattern at line 1817), two `nn.Parameter` scalars per block × 12 blocks = 24 scalars (+0.003%), and a single branch in `SwiGLUZeroInitFeedForward.forward` to compute the blended `W_up_eff` and `W_down_eff`. The plan touches `models/components.py` (the FFN module already lives there at `SwiGLUZeroInitFeedForward` line 80), `models/llm.py` (block-to-block prev_W plumbing), and `configs/llm_config.py` (one new flag + one new `Tiny1M3MFFNShareConfig` subclass). Well under 200 LoC.
- **Transfer-risk: med, justified — passing.** ALBERT at BERT-base/large/xxlarge (110M-235M) is the largest scale where the *partial-tying family* has empirical wins; Universal Transformers at <100M. Both share *all* parameters, 206 shares only W_up + W_down. The lever is novel at <100M, and the in-repo full-layer-tying null is the empirical envelope that 206 must beat on a *narrower* axis. `med` is correct (low would be wrong — the lever has not been validated at <100M; high would be wrong — Universal Transformer family is well-validated and partial tying is a weaker regularizer than the published analogs).
- **Family prior — passing.** 021 value-residual (in-repo WIN Δ=−0.034) is the V-side cross-block carry that demonstrates cross-block *information propagation* can pay off at 0.94M. 206 is on a different matrix and uses a *projection-sharing* (not residual-stream) mechanism, but the family precedent shows the depth-conditional axis binds at this tier. The closed full-layer-tying null is the *opposing* prior — and the 206 bet (narrower tying on the largest FFN matrices specifically) is the cleanest way to localize *which* part of full tying was the binding constraint.
- **Queue crowding — passing.** The 5+ in-flight tying/carry ideas (188 K/V-share, 197 W_O-share, 204 cross-block-attn-score-share) are all on the *attention side* or on *residual-stream side* (021 V-residual). 206 is the only FFN-projection-sharing lever in the queue — it isolates a *fresh* sub-family (FFN-matrix tying). Not a duplicate of 188 (KV), 197 (W_O), 204 (scores), or 021 (V-residual).

### Findings (to be addressed by the code-implementer in plan.md, none blocking)

1. **Pass/fail bar must be made concrete in plan.md.** `idea.md` does not name a numeric pass-bar or a target Δ-val. The plan must specify (a) cache-anchored baseline reference (e.g. recent box-keyed ctrl mean from `autoresearch/baseline-cache.json` + 3 same-session ctrls), (b) WIN band (`trt_val ≤ ctrl − 0.01`, well above box noise), (c) NULL band (`|trt_val − ctrl| ≤ 0.01` — inconclusive, not promoted per ONE-SEED-ONLY), (d) DRIFT band (`trt_val > ctrl + 0.01` — closes the FFN-tying axis). The a-priori expectation: the closed full-layer-tying null is the *envelope*; 206 is a weaker regularizer, so an even smaller or null effect is the central case. A `Δ ∈ [−0.01, +0.005]` prior is honest.
2. **α trajectory as secondary signal.** Per-block `α_up_b`, `α_down_b` should be dumped at end-of-training (24 scalars). The 188 / 197 family pattern is "α stays at 0 ⇒ the binding constraint is not in this axis ⇒ informative close." Both α-stays-zero and α-moves-off-zero are informative; a 206 NULL where α stays at 0 across all 24 scalars is a *cleaner* null than 188/197's respective α-stays-0 reads.
3. **`use_fire_pe` preservation in the new config subclass.** Recent config-subclass patterns (closed.md `WIN w/ caveat` entries for 021/023/024/025) have occasionally dropped `use_fire_pe=True` from the inheritance. The new `Tiny1M3MFFNShareConfig` (or whatever the implementer names it) must inherit from the existing tiny1m3m config and *only* flip `use_cross_block_ffn_share=True`, to avoid the well-known subclass-override trap.
4. **Step-0 byte-identity smoke must include the new build.** Standard plan § "Step-0 smoke" should add the `use_cross_block_ffn_share=True` build to the fp32 max-abs-diff < 1e-6 verification across all 12 blocks. The `α ≈ 4.5e-5` math makes this trivially bit-identical, but the smoke guards against the `prev_W_up` plumbing accidentally mutating the parameter state at init.
5. **First-block (b=0) edge case.** `b=0` has no previous block. The plan should specify how `prev_W_up` / `prev_W_down` are initialized for block 0 (recommended: `prev_W_up = W_up_0` itself, so the blend at b=0 is `W_up_eff = W_up_0` regardless of α — but `α_0` will then have no gradient direction; either keep `α_0` as a learnable scalar with the trivial blend, or freeze it at 0). The 188 pattern at `models/llm.py:1817` is the reference.

### Verdict: approve.

Mechanism is sound, source citations real, dedup clean, LoC budget met, transfer-risk `med` justified against ALBERT/UT scale evidence, family precedent (021 V-residual WIN) supports the depth-conditional axis. Findings 1-5 are plan.md-level operationalization (pass bar, α dump, config inheritance, step-0 smoke, b=0 edge case) — none of them block `needs-plan`. Reset `round` to 1 for the code gate.
