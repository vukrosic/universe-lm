## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: revise

- **Closed family, but the re-pitch is too soft.** `closed.md` explicitly lists "MHA vs GQA, MLA, Tied QK" as a closed arch-sweep axis at tiny1m3m, and `043-mla` was already taste-rejected citing that exact closure. MQA sits at the `group=1` corner of GQA, so the gated variant re-tests a closed lever in a thin disguise. The defense "but we tested fixed groups, not a *learnable per-head blend*" is theoretically interesting but mechanistically weak: if MHA and MQA are both null-leaning at 0.94M, the smooth interpolation between them cannot beat the better endpoint — convex combination of two ~equal-loss points is still ~equal-loss, with extra params (gate vectors + unused-but-allocated shared K/V projections, ~10% param overhead at 0.94M) eating any win.
- **Leverage at our scale is a phantom.** K/V parameters at H=4, d_model=64 are ~32K total — MQA's 75% cut saves ~24K, i.e. ~2.5% of model. At 0.94M the binding constraint is not K/V count; it's hidden-state bandwidth / d_head = 16 per head. The gated form exists to give the optimizer a per-head choice, but the choice has no useful gradient direction at this scale.
- **Crisp-bet problem.** Current one-liner: "the per-head gate will find an optimal mix that beats both pure MHA and pure MQA." That isn't a hypothesis, it's a hope. The definition gate can't run a test against "find an optimal mix" — there has to be a directional prediction (gates move toward >0.5 in ≥N heads? gates collapse to 0? loss delta sign?), and right now there isn't one.
- **Queue crowding.** Active queue holds 179-anti-causal-subheads (tasting), 180-qk-logit-conv (needs-taste), 181-cross-head-rmsnorm (needs-taste), 182-per-head-window (needs-taste) — four other attention-axis ideas. Adding a fifth on a closed family is the textbook "revise (diversify)" pattern even if each one is individually fine.
- **How to close the taste gap.** Pick one of these three and make it the bet:
  1. **Probe-only re-pitch.** Sharpen to: "after tiny1m3m, the gates β_h move *somewhere* off zero in ≥1 head, OR they collapse to zero (which confirms MHA is the per-head optimum and closes the gated family with mechanistic evidence)." This is a testable, falsifiable bet; a clean null is informative. The mechanism and code can stay as drafted. Drop the "might lower val loss" framing entirely.
  2. **Re-cast as a transfer study at a scale where K/V actually matters.** Per protocol we run tiny1m3m only, so this direction is `reject`-bound unless the orchestrator carves out a Phase-2 budget — but worth flagging.
  3. **Pivot away from MQA entirely.** Use the gated-blend machinery on a different axis the closed arch-sweep didn't touch (e.g. gated *output* projection, gated Q/O sharing) — keeps the β-form identity trick but applies it somewhere novel.

- **Information value, the saving grace.** Option 1 above has real info value: a clean null (gates stay at 0) mechanistically explains the closed arch-sweep's null and closes the GQA family for good. A win (gates move) is interesting but likely small in val-loss terms. That's the right framing to bring back to the miner.
- **What the miner must produce in r2.** (a) one sharp sentence of "we expect X because Y" — direction of β_h movement matters, not the loss number; (b) explicit acceptance that this is a probe, not a lever, and what we learn from each outcome; (c) confirmation the implementation overhead (~10% extra params during the head-local phase) is acceptable for the info gained.
