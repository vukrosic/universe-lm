## r1 — 2026-06-15 — verdict: approve

- **Source is real and current.** Shazeer 2019 (arXiv:1911.02150, MQA) and Ainslie 2023 (arXiv:2305.13245, GQA) both verified citations; mechanism family well-validated at ≥100M (MQA at PaLM-540B, GQA at Llama-2 7B/13B/70B). The gated-blend form is novel but rides on a vetted family.
- **Mechanism, not a hyperparameter.** β init 0 ⇒ `K_h = K_h_local` exactly, so the forward at step 0 is the MHA baseline's forward. β is a *learnable per-KV-head scalar* that gives the optimizer a smooth interpolation axis between MHA and MQA. This is a structural lever, not an LR/init-constant knob.
- **Not a mathematical duplicate of the closed arch-sweep.** `closed.md:23` lists "MHA vs GQA" as a closed axis, but the closed sweep tested *fixed group sizes* (H, 2, …) — discrete points on the convex hull. The gated form is a *smooth, learnable per-KV-head blend* the discrete sweep cannot represent. Even if β stays at 0 (the "no useful gradient direction" outcome), the answer upgrades the existing empirical null to a mechanistic one with a causal explanation — "the per-head optimum is MHA, GQA interpolation has no useful gradient direction at this tier." That is information, not a re-run of the closed test. The r2 bet (β moves off zero in ≥1 head, OR collapses to zero everywhere) is the right falsifiable framing.
- **Probe framing is sound and is what makes this survive the closed-axis question.** r1's "convex combination of two ~equal-loss points can't beat either endpoint" critique was the right objection against a lever framing. r2 drops the lever framing entirely and reframes as a *mechanistic probe* — the artifact being measured is the β_h trajectory, not val loss. Both outcomes are informative; a noisy half-move is a third teachable outcome. This is the correct resolution of r1's "crisp-bet problem."
- **Implementable in < 200 LoC, and already done.** A `nn.Linear(d_model, d_model)` shared K/V per block, two `nn.Parameter(n_kv_heads)` gate vectors per block, one branch in `MultiHeadAttention.forward` to mix `K_local + β·(K_shared − K_local)`. Verified in working tree: `models/layers.py:1231-1232, 2061-2108, 2730-2757`, `models/llm.py:313, 698, 988`, `configs/llm_config.py:6182 (Tiny1M3MMQAGatedConfig)`. Net diff well under 200 LoC.
- **tiny1m3m-only, seed 42.** No reference to screen20m, full ladder, or multi-seed. The probe is informationally gated by single-seed reading (sub-noise effect = inconclusive, not real).
- **Transfer-risk: low, justified.** MQA at PaLM-540B and GQA at Llama-2 7B/13B/70B are direct validations; the gated-blend novelty is small. The `low` tag is defensible against the citation.
- **Falsifiable pass/fail bar.** Primary signal: per-head β_k_h, β_v_h at end-of-training (the r2 spec says 96 scalars — see minor finding below). PASS = ≥1 β_h moves measurably off zero in any block; PASS (informational) = all β_h collapse to or stay at zero; null = both/noisy-half-move. Val-loss column is recorded but explicitly secondary. This is a sharp, runnable bar.
- **Param overhead accepted, correctly bounded.** +10.5% (98,328 params at tiny1m3m) is on the heavier side for a taste slot, but the r2 trade — clean mechanistic signal vs a 10%-heavier baseline — is sound. The miner has explicitly accepted this; the 10% is paid for information, not for a loss delta.
- **Queue crowding, re-evaluated.** 179/180/181/182 are loss-Δ-driven levers; this is the *only* idea in the queue that produces a mechanistic close-or-keep signal for the entire GQA family. They're complementary, not redundant — the crowding argument cuts the *other* way here.

### Findings (to be addressed by the reviser / code-implementer, in plan.md)

1. **Per-head β dump is the primary signal.** The runner must record final `β_k_h`, `β_v_h` (per KV-head, per block) at end-of-training and write them to `results.json` (or `evidence.md`). The taste.md flagged this as the most critical downstream concern — without the dump, the probe collapses to a 10%-heavier baseline run. Plan.md must include this hook. *(Naming: file:section to add — `plan.md` § "Runner hooks" must enumerate the per-block, per-KV-head final β tensors as a recorded output alongside val_loss and val_acc.)*
2. **Step-0 byte-identity verification.** The mechanism claim is "β=0 ⇒ byte-identical to MHA baseline at step 0." The plan's standard step-0 smoke (max-abs-diff < 1e-6 across all 12 blocks) must include the `use_mqa_gated=True` build and explicitly compare against the no-flag baseline. If the shared K/V projection allocation mutates the parameter state at all, this surfaces it. *(Naming: same plan section as the step-0 smoke.)*
3. **Minor: scalar count.** The idea.md says "Gate vectors: H = 4 per (K, V) per block = 96 total." At tiny1m3m the gate is per-`n_kv_heads` (not per-`n_heads`), and `n_kv_heads = 2` in the config, so the actual count is 2 per (K, V) per block = 4 per block = **48 total**, not 96. Doesn't change LoC feasibility or the informational value (the gate is still negligible) but the spec text is off by 2×. Plan.md and evidence.md should use the correct count.
4. **Val-loss is informational only.** Plan.md's pass-bar section must state this explicitly: a small win is interesting, a null (β stays at 0) is informative, and the WIN bar should be on the β trajectory, not on Δval. Otherwise the code-implementer / runner could default to a val-loss-only A/B, defeating the probe.
5. **Per-KV-head vs per-head.** Implementation correctly uses `n_kv_heads` (groups of 2 query heads share a β at n_heads=4, n_kv_heads=2). Plan.md should call this out so reviewers don't mistake a 2-scalar/group gate for a 4-scalar/head gate — the optimizer's resolution is per-KV-group, not per-query-head, which slightly weakens outcome (a) but doesn't invalidate it.
