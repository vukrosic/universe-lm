## r1 — 2026-06-11 — verdict: revise

- **No sharp bet — explicit "stability lever even if not a headline gain" is a vibe-pitch.** The prompt is clear: an idea needs one sentence of "we expect X because Y". As written, the predicted outcome is *unfalsifiable*: a sub-noise stability improvement at tiny1m3m/seed-42/~92 steps reads as **inconclusive, not real** — and "inconclusive" is exactly the failure mode the leverage + info-value criteria are meant to reject. Replace with a numeric Δ-val-loss bet against a *named* baseline (e.g. "−0.005 to −0.02 vs ctrl with global clip removed"), or state the *specific* instability AGC fires on and why a clean A/B will detect it.
- **The bet collides with an existing repo lever: `configs/llm_config.py:580` sets `grad_clip: float = 1.0` and `training/trainer.py:574,873` already calls `torch.nn.utils.clip_grad_norm_(model.parameters(), config.grad_clip)`.** AGC is *per-tensor adaptive grad clipping*; the repo already runs *global* grad clipping. Stacking AGC on top of global clip at threshold 1.0 means AGC almost never fires (global clip already swallows the spikes), so the A/B measures noise. The plan must be: **(a) remove the global clip and replace with AGC** (clean mechanism A/B), or **(b) explicitly motivate why per-tensor on top of global is non-redundant with a measured baseline**. Without that, the experiment is structurally info-free.
- **"tiny1m3m has shown that a few bad steps can dominate the run" — citation needed.** This is the load-bearing claim for the entire pitch and there's no evidence behind it. Was that step-domination *with* `grad_clip=1.0` active? If yes, that's a strong sharpening — name the run, link the loss trace. If you mean FoX/Softpick NaN'd (020/022), those were *row-renorm* blow-ups inside the attention block, not optimizer-update magnitude blowups; AGC would not have caught them (the NaN entered the forward pass before the optimizer step). Either way, the motivation needs a concrete pointer.
- **Transfer-risk: med is under-defended.** Source is NFNets — a vision paper whose entire raison d'être was *replacing BatchNorm* (not present in this LM). The "AGC enables large batches + strong aug" headline is two regimes we don't run: tiny1m3m is small-batch and no augmentation. The LM-side evidence (post-2021 transformer pretraining adopting AGC as a key lever) is essentially absent — most LLM trainers stay on global norm clip. Re-pitch needs at least one LM/transformer reference where AGC won over global clip, or own the transfer-risk explicitly with a mechanistic argument that doesn't lean on the BN-replacement narrative.
- **Portfolio context (not a rejection axis on its own).** Optimizer / pre-step-shaping wave is at saturation: 7 done (5 NULL, 2 WIN: 011-cautious-lion, 015-moonlight-rms), 031-034/037-039 all queued at `needs-taste`, 036-lamb and 040-adafactor already taste-rejected this week. A sharpened AGC bet (clip-replacement A/B, named instability) can earn a slot in this wave; the current "safety valve, maybe helps" framing cannot.
